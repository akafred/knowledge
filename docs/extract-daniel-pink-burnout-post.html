Burnout & Recovery
Burnout is caused not by the presence of stress but by the absence of recovery.

practical · aphorism / social media post — A reframing intervention disguised as a pithy two-line observation. Practical in intent: it aims to change how the reader diagnoses their own burnout and, implicitly, what they do about it.

Arguments

Burnout is caused by lack of rest, not by stress itself. strength: low
Explicit premises:
  • Your brain isn't burning out because you're stressed.
  • It's burning out because it never gets to stop.
Implicit premises:
  • Stress and lack of rest are meaningfully distinct — you can be stressed and still recover if you get downtime.
  • Burnout has a single dominant cause (or at least the popularly assumed cause is wrong).
  • The reader is currently attributing their burnout to stress rather than to lack of recovery.
Inferences:
  • If you address rest/recovery rather than stress reduction, you will address burnout more effectively.

Questions addressed

  • ✅ What causes burnout?
    Lack of cognitive rest, not stress per se.
  • ⭕ What should you do about burnout?
    Implied: create opportunities for the brain to stop. No specific guidance given.
