Entur AS' innlegg — KI-løypa: Slik vurderer og innfører vi KI-verktøy i Entur

Entur AS · article · 2026-04-16 source 🖥️

Entur has developed a structured five-stage governance process ('KI-løypa') for evaluating, piloting, and managing AI tools responsibly, balancing the pursuit of efficiency gains against privacy, security, regulatory compliance, and ethical obligations inherent in their public-sector mandate.

practical · corporate LinkedIn post / process overview — A public-facing organizational communication describing Entur's internal governance process for evaluating and adopting AI tools, structured as a five-stage pipeline.

Structure

PartRole
Opening framingEstablishes the dual nature of AI: high potential value (decision support, efficiency, productivity) alongside fundamental governance questions (privacy, security, transparency, regulation, ethics).
Stage 1: Forslag og innspill (Proposals and input)Describes the intake mechanism — ideas come from anyone in the organization, bottom-up.
Stage 2: Kost/nyttevurdering (Cost/benefit assessment)Introduces the first gate: a future system owner and AI coordinator filter out low-value initiatives early to avoid wasting resources.
Stage 3: Risiko og informasjonsklassifisering (Risk and information classification)Introduces the compliance gate: a cross-functional group assesses data types, risk class (including EU AI Act classification), and can reject tools deemed too risky.
Stage 4: Pilotering og eventuell innføring (Piloting and possible adoption)Describes practical validation through pilot testing before broader rollout.
Stage 5: Forvaltning over tid (Governance over time)Emphasizes that adoption is not the end — ongoing monitoring ensures continued safe and relevant use.
Closing philosophy and call to actionFrames the overall approach as 'ansvarlig digitalisering' (responsible digitalization) rather than technology maximalism, and invites community dialogue.

Key terms

  • KI-løypa Agent Governance (Internal)
    Entur's structured five-stage governance pipeline for evaluating, testing, and managing AI tools — from proposal through ongoing stewardship. Literally 'the AI track/trail'.
  • kost/nyttevurdering Agent Governance (Internal)
    A cost/benefit assessment performed by a future system owner and AI coordinator to filter out AI initiatives that lack sufficient potential value.
  • informasjonsklassifisering Agent Governance (Internal)
    Classification of the type of information an AI tool will process, used to determine risk level and regulatory requirements.
  • EUs KI-forordning AI Regulation & Governance (External)
    The EU AI Act — used as one of the frameworks for determining the risk class of AI tools under evaluation.
  • ansvarlig digitalisering Agent Governance (Internal)
    Responsible digitalization — the guiding philosophy that AI should serve as a means to better services for employees, users, and society, not technology adoption for its own sake.
  • systemeier
    The designated future owner of an AI tool within the organization, responsible for its cost/benefit assessment and ongoing governance.
  • KI-koordinator
    An organizational role that works alongside system owners to evaluate AI tool proposals in the cost/benefit stage.

Arguments

Organizations (especially public-sector ones) need a structured governance process for AI tool adoption. strength: medium
Explicit premises:
  • AI offers significant potential benefits (decision support, efficiency, productivity).
  • AI also raises fundamental questions about value, privacy, security, transparency, regulation, ethics, and mission.
  • Without a structured process, organizations risk adopting tools that increase complexity without creating value, or that violate privacy/regulatory requirements.
Implicit premises:
  • Ad hoc AI adoption is the default mode in many organizations and leads to poor outcomes.
  • The risks of AI tools are not always apparent without structured assessment.
  • Public-sector organizations face higher accountability standards than private companies.
Inferences:
  • The argument moves from 'AI has both potential and risk' to 'therefore we need a structured process' — the link is plausible but the post provides no evidence of what went wrong without such a process.
Entur's five-stage KI-løypa is an adequate governance framework for AI tool adoption. strength: low
Explicit premises:
  • Stage 1 captures proposals from across the organization.
  • Stage 2 filters for value before investing resources.
  • Stage 3 assesses risk and regulatory compliance (including EU AI Act).
  • Stage 4 validates through piloting.
  • Stage 5 ensures ongoing governance.
Implicit premises:
  • These five stages cover all the relevant governance dimensions.
  • The assessment criteria at each stage are well-defined and consistently applied.
  • The cross-functional vurderingsgruppe (assessment group) has the necessary competence.
Inferences:
  • The stages are described at a high level — the adequacy of the framework depends entirely on implementation details not shared in the post.

Narratives

  • A developer discovers a code-assistance AI tool and submits it to KI-løypa, where it progresses through cost/benefit evaluation, risk assessment, piloting, and potentially into managed use.
    → Makes the abstract five-stage process concrete and relatable by grounding it in a specific, recognizable scenario (developer finding a coding AI tool).
  • Entur positions itself as a thoughtful, responsible organization navigating the AI landscape deliberately rather than chasing hype.
    → Serves as employer branding and stakeholder communication — signals maturity and trustworthiness to the public, partners, and potential hires.

Questions addressed

  • ✅ How does Entur evaluate whether an AI tool creates real value or just adds complexity?
    Through a cost/benefit assessment in Stage 2, conducted by a future system owner and AI coordinator.
  • ✅ How does Entur ensure AI tool adoption complies with privacy, security, and regulatory requirements?
    Through risk and information classification in Stage 3, including EU AI Act classification, with authority to reject tools deemed too risky.
  • ✅ How does Entur ensure AI tools remain appropriate after initial adoption?
    Through ongoing governance in Stage 5, with regular follow-up on safety, responsibility, and continued relevance.
  • ⭕ What specific criteria are used at each stage to make accept/reject decisions?
  • ⭕ How effective has the KI-løypa been in practice — how many tools have passed through it and with what outcomes?
