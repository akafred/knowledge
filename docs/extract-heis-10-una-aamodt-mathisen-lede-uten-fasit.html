Propositions

  • descriptiveThe reason a cross-functional team exists is precisely because the solution is not knowable in advance by any one person.
  • prescriptiveA leader must publicly acknowledge that she does not know the answer to the question she is asking the team to solve.
  • prescriptiveThe leader's role under uncertainty has three distinct phases: detective-work listening on arrival, hypothesis-shaped direction-setting, and closing the learning loop afterward.
  • descriptivedevelopedDirection-setting (retning) and team autonomy are not opposites; they are mutually constitutive — autonomy without shared direction produces drift, direction without autonomy produces literal compliance.

  • descriptiveAs uncertainty rises, the required cadence and quality of stakeholder communication rises proportionally — and engineers/builders systematically under-invest in this because they are absorbed in the work.
  • prescriptiveTranslate a higher-level strategy into your own area's language as your first concrete act of direction-setting; do not wait for it to be handed down at your altitude.
  • prescriptiveWhen you observe team behavior that signals defense or distrust (e.g., being kicked out of a Slack channel), exercise restraint — do not 'arrest' people for it; treat it as data about leadership scar tissue, not an offense.
  • descriptiveLarge organizations resemble each other across the public/private divide more than they resemble small organizations in their own sector; complexity of structure and decision lines is the dominant variable, not sector.
  • prescriptiveTools and frameworks (tight/loose, OKR, autonomous teams) only work when 'made your own' — adapted to organizational maturity, sector, and the team's experience with prior methods.
  • descriptiveThe good solutions live inside difficult conversations, not around them; friction in cross-cultural merger work (e.g., Kron + Storebrand) is the medium of value creation, not an obstacle to it.
  • descriptiveMathisen's leadership style — which she finds hard to describe — is in fact highly legible from outside: gathering internally, transparent externally, generating pride-of-employment.

Arguments

The leader's job under genuine cross-functional uncertainty is to set direction with a publicly stated hypothesis and to actively make space for the team to outperform her own imagination.
Slow orientation (listening + identifying what works) is the load-bearing first move because it produces a direction that the existing team can recognize as theirs, not the new boss's imposition.
The friction is not a sign of failed integration; it is the medium through which the better combined service is being designed, and shutting it down would destroy the value.
