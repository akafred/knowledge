What we lose when AI writes for us

Afonso Franco · email · 2026-05-25 Reader 🖥️

AI SlopCognitive SurrenderWriting as Thinking
When we let AI write for us, we don't merely outsource a technical task — we outsource the cognitive struggle through which we clarify beliefs, form judgment, and constitute the self, and at civilizational scale this risks converging humanity not just on a shared style but on shared thought.

practical · mixed — Mixed expository/argumentative essay with a strong practical thrust: Franco wants the reader to change behavior — to keep writing from a blank page, to use LLMs as co-creator rather than ghostwriter. The argumentative spine (writing-is-thinking; outsourcing writing outsources selfhood) is in service of a prescriptive conclusion.

Key terms

  • Self Writing as Thinking
    The crystallized set of beliefs, judgments, and identity that emerges through the act of articulating one's thoughts in writing; not a pre-existing essence but something constituted by the struggle of expression.
  • Cognitive struggle Writing as Thinking
    The slow, uncomfortable mental work of turning vague feelings and ideas into coherent thoughts — distinct from a 'technical barrier' that AI may legitimately remove.
  • AI slop AI Slop
    The recognizable homogenized LLM style — short sentences, false dichotomies, anaphora/epistrophe, concrete-to-abstract pivots, em-dashes — that Franco can now 'smell' and that has become 'humanity's writing style'.
  • Linguistic convergence (at civilizational scale) Writing as Thinking
    A society-wide collapse of rhetorical diversity into a single LLM-derived style, with the further implication that thought itself converges along with rhetoric.
  • Co-creator Writing as Thinking
    An acceptable mode of LLM use in which the human remains 'in the driver's seat,' goes for walks when stuck, and uses the LLM for sparring/improvement — contrasted with using LLMs to generate the text wholesale.
  • Writing Writing as Thinking
    Not the output but the struggle: the slow process of turning feelings into coherent thoughts; equivalent to thinking ('writing is thinking').

Arguments

Letting AI write for you erodes selfhood. strength: medium
Explicit premises:
  • Writing is the process by which we clarify beliefs, form judgment, and shape the self.
  • AI writing for you removes the cognitive struggle of that process.
  • Removing the cognitive struggle removes the mechanism of self-formation.
Implicit premises:
  • The Self is constituted by, not just expressed in, writing (a constructivist theory of selfhood).
  • No other cognitive activity (speaking, drawing, deliberating in conversation, etc.) can substitute for writing in this role.
  • AI-assisted or AI-edited writing is categorically different from AI-generated writing — only the latter is corrosive.
Inferences:
  • If selfhood is constituted by effortful articulation, then heavy LLM use across other articulation domains (slide-making, email drafting, slack messages) should be similarly corrosive — yet Franco doesn't extend the argument there.
  • If the Self is built through struggle, then practices that restore the struggle (Zettelkasten, longhand journaling, 'writing without LLM' rituals) become not preferences but ethical hygiene.
We are heading toward civilizational cognitive convergence. strength: low
Explicit premises:
  • LLM style has become 'humanity's writing style.'
  • Stylistic homogenization is empirically visible (LinkedIn, articles, books).
  • If we converge around rhetoric, we converge around thought.
Implicit premises:
  • Style and thought are tightly coupled — you cannot adopt a borrowed rhetoric without adopting (some of) its conceptual scaffolding.
  • Reading and writing in a homogenized style trains the reader's cognition into the same shape (a sort of Sapir-Whorf claim applied to LLM idiom).
  • The pre-LLM diversity of style reflected a pre-LLM diversity of thought (rather than, say, class/access markers).
Inferences:
  • If style trains thought, then deliberate stylistic resistance (writing 'against' the LLM idiom) is itself a cognitive intervention.
  • If true, this argument applies symmetrically to any era of stylistic dominance (e.g., AP style, MBA-speak) — but Franco treats LLM convergence as uniquely civilizational.
You should keep writing from a blank page and use LLMs only as a co-creator/sparring partner. strength: medium
Explicit premises:
  • Writing from a blank page is the mechanism of selfhood.
  • Outsourcing it loses the Self.
  • Co-creator use keeps the human 'in the driver's seat.'
Implicit premises:
  • The line between 'sparring partner' and 'ghostwriter' is in practice drawable by the user — a phenomenological boundary the essay does not specify.
  • Self-confidence and patience are individually cultivable goods rather than scaffolding the social environment is currently removing.
Inferences:
  • If the co-creator/ghostwriter line is hard to draw operationally, then well-intentioned users will gradually slide across it without noticing — which would mean Franco's prescription is unstable in practice.

    → Performative demonstration: the essay enacts its own thesis. The narrative implicitly supports the claim that the cognitive struggle is recoverable and valuable.

    → Meta-rhetorical demonstration that the techniques are now legible — supports the empirical claim that the style is pervasive and recognizable.
  • The blank page reframed at the end as a mirror.

Questions addressed

  • ✅ What is the morally relevant difference between AI removing a technical barrier and AI doing the writing for you?
    The former removes friction that doesn't form the Self; the latter removes the cognitive struggle that does.
  • ✅ Why does it matter that everyone is starting to sound the same?
    Because sounding the same will eventually mean thinking the same — civilizational cognitive convergence.
  • ✅ What should we do about it?
    Use LLMs as co-creator/sparring partner; preserve the practice of writing from a blank page.
  • ⭕ Where exactly is the line between AI-as-editor/sparring partner (acceptable) and AI-as-author (corrosive)?
  • ⭕ Why is writing — specifically — the privileged site of selfhood, rather than speech, drawing, building, deliberating with friends, or longhand journaling?
  • ⭕ Is the claim that style-convergence entails thought-convergence empirical, conceptual, or a Sapir-Whorf-style hypothesis? What would falsify it?
  • ⭕ Were pre-LLM writers actually forming richer selves through writing, or is Franco idealizing a baseline that was already homogenized by AP style, MBA-speak, SEO, etc.?

Gaps

  • incompleteFranco never distinguishes operationally between AI-as-editor and AI-as-author. He gestures at 'co-creator' and 'sparring partner' but offers no test for when LLM involvement has crossed from helping you think to thinking for you. Since the whole prescription hinges on this line, its absence is the essay's central structural weakness.
    Read next: Andy Matuschak's notes on evergreen-note writing and 'understanding requires effortful engagement'; Sönke Ahrens, How to Take Smart Notes (the operational case for note-writing as thinking); literature on cognitive offloading (Risko & Gilbert) for empirical traction on which kinds of offloading degrade what kinds of cognition.
  • incompleteThe implicit theory of selfhood is constructivist (writing constitutes the Self) but Franco never engages alternatives: that selfhood is constituted in speech (Charles Taylor, dialogical self), in action (Aristotelian), in narrative across many modalities (Ricoeur), or that the 'Self' being expressed by his authorial gallery is a cultivated public persona rather than an inner essence.
    Read next: Charles Taylor, Sources of the Self; Paul Ricoeur, Oneself as Another; Sönke Ahrens on writing-as-thinking; and contra: Cal Newport / classical-rhetoric arguments for speech and dialectic as primary.
  • incompleteThe argument from stylistic convergence to cognitive convergence is the most consequential claim in the essay and is unsupported. Franco asserts it ('we converge around rhetoric, what happens then?') but provides no mechanism. A Sapir-Whorf-style claim is doing the work uncited.
    Read next: Linguistic relativity literature (Lera Boroditsky, Guy Deutscher); studies of LLM influence on user vocabulary and reasoning (e.g., recent work on 'epistemic homogenization' in LLM-assisted writing); Stolen Focus (Hari) on attention-environment shaping cognition.
  • incompleteThe argument privileges writing as the unique site of Self-formation without explaining why other effortful articulation practices (drawing, coding, deliberating aloud, longhand journaling, even careful prompting) cannot substitute. This matters because if other practices CAN substitute, the prescription changes from 'keep writing from a blank page' to 'preserve some effortful articulation practice.'
    Read next: Embodied-cognition work (Andy Clark, Supersizing the Mind); the diary tradition (Sarah Manguso, Ongoingness) for non-public writing as Self-formation; sketchbook/visual-thinking traditions (Dan Roam).
  • incompleteNo engagement with the possibility that LLM-mediated reflection can be MORE rigorous than solo blank-page writing for some people — e.g., neurodivergent users for whom externalized dialogue scaffolds rather than replaces thought; or that writing from a blank page is itself a culturally specific Western Romantic ideal.
    Read next: Literature on extended cognition; disability/neurodivergence and AI-assistance; the rhetoric tradition (which privileges dialogue and topoi over solitary blank-page composition).
  • illogicalSelf-undermining performance: Franco demonstrates LLM-style techniques 'on his own' (the anaphora caveat) to prove he can do them without an LLM. But this concedes that the techniques themselves are not the marker of AI authorship — humans use them too. If the stylistic tells are not in fact diagnostic, the empirical claim ('humanity's writing style') is weaker than asserted.
    Read next: Studies on inter-rater reliability of human/AI text detection; the history of these rhetorical figures (which long predate LLMs).
  • uninformedThe essay treats LLM-induced stylistic homogenization as historically novel. Prior waves of stylistic dominance (AP style, MBA-speak, SEO writing, business-jargon, Strunk-and-White) produced their own convergences without producing the cognitive collapse Franco fears — suggesting style-to-thought transmission may be weaker than claimed.
    Read next: History of plain-style movements (Orwell, 'Politics and the English Language'); style guide history; sociolinguistics of register convergence.
