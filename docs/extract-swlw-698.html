Arguments

Software leaders must fundamentally rethink what they own and how they lead, because AI makes strategic thinking directly visible through domain models. strength: high
Explicit premises:
  • Leaders now own their domain model's understanding, not just outcomes
  • The model is a mirror of strategic thinking
  • Complexity and information fog no longer serve as shields
Implicit premises:
  • Before AI, leaders could plausibly claim that organizational complexity made complete understanding impossible
  • AI-powered domain models create a new form of accountability that did not previously exist
Inferences:
  • Leaders who relied on organizational complexity as cover will be exposed
  • The quality of leadership will become more measurable and transparent
AI will make the concierge model of customer service universal, merging service and commerce. strength: medium
Explicit premises:
  • The concierge model (continuous, proactive, personalized) was previously only feasible for high-ARPU luxury businesses due to human labor costs
  • AI removes the cost constraint that limited this model
Implicit premises:
  • Customers universally prefer personalized, proactive service over generic, reactive service
  • The economic logic that previously separated service from sales was a constraint, not a feature
Inferences:
  • Companies that treat customer service as a cost center rather than a revenue channel will lose to those who adopt the AI concierge model
Using AI is table stakes; competitive advantage requires AI to change the end product. strength: high
Explicit premises:
  • If you use AI at the same level as everyone else, you have no advantage by definition
  • The only question is how AI changes what you deliver to customers
Implicit premises:
  • AI adoption across the industry is fast enough that process efficiency gains from AI will be competed away quickly
  • Product differentiation through AI is harder and therefore more defensible than process optimization through AI
Inferences:
  • Internal AI adoption without product impact is a waste of differentiation opportunity
