Is AI Doom Going Out of Style?

Key terms

  • vibeshift AI Labor Disruption
    A collective, felt change in the dominant cultural narrative about AI and employment — moving from doom/mass-unemployment framing toward nuance and even optimism
  • Jevons' Paradox Jevons Paradox
    When technology makes a resource (here: labor in a task) cheaper to use, total consumption of that resource increases because new demand is unlocked — applied to AI automation of jobs
  • demand elasticity Jevons Paradox
    The degree to which reducing the cost of a task via AI creates new demand for that task and adjacent tasks, rather than simply eliminating jobs
  • seats to tokens AI Unit Economics
    The business model shift from per-user SaaS subscriptions to usage-based pricing where AI agents consume tokens, removing the ceiling on per-customer revenue
  • structurally unreliable narrators
    Silicon Valley AI builders whose financial incentives (IPOs, valuations) and professional blind spots (not being economists) make their predictions about labor market impacts systematically biased

Gaps

  • omissionNo engagement with the counter-argument that AI is categorically different from prior automation. Jevons' Paradox applied to computing, but AI automates cognition itself — the piece never addresses why the historical analogy should hold when the substrate being automated is fundamentally different.
  • tensionConflates elite commentary vibeshift with actual public narrative change. The piece acknowledges polls remain negative but does not reconcile this with its thesis that a meaningful shift is underway.
  • omissionSoftware engineering job data is presented without controlling for what kinds of roles are growing. If AI is creating demand for AI-adjacent roles while displacing traditional development, the headline number masks distributional harm.
    closed byAI Companies Are Hiring More — Delivers the distributional decomposition the gap asks for: AI-exposed sectors (tech, finance) losing ~28,000 jobs/month while the overall economy adds ~113,000, showing how headline optimism can mask sectoral displacement.
  • omissionThe Anthropic revenue numbers ($9B to $44B ARR) are presented without questioning sustainability or margin structure beyond the current moment. Revenue growth rate =/= sustainable business.
  • underdevelopedKlein's insight that 8 million displaced workers may be harder to help than 80 million is introduced but not developed. This is potentially the most important policy point in the piece and gets one sentence.
