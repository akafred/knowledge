5) Vidar Moe: Det eneste Vidar vil er å ha det lett

Vidar Moe's core engineering-leadership philosophy is 'gjøre det lett og rett' (make it easy and right) — a Toyota-Way-inflected craft and leadership stance in which you lead an organization most powerfully not by exhortation but by patiently building the tooling, frameworks, fagtid, hiring filters, fixed-term leadership rotation, and shared decision arenas that make the right thing the path of least resistance; the episode's ironic title ('the only thing Vidar wants is to have it easy') inverts this — Moe wants the work to be easy precisely because he is willing to do the very hard, decade-long, mostly invisible work of removing friction from doing the right thing, and his current concern with AI agents is the natural next chapter of the same project.

practical · narrative — Long-form career-and-methodology interview. The form is conversational-narrative: Truls and Audun draw out Moe's 20-year arc at SpareBank 1 Utvikling — from consultant to insourced developer, from monolith to vertical teams, from no formal leadership to fixed-term faglederrolle and distributed personnel management. The intellectual content is practical/operational (Lean/Toyota, OKR, ADRs, fagtid, pair programming, scientific method as the unit of software development), but the delivery is autobiographical. Almost no abstract theorising; everything is grounded in 'da gjorde vi X fordi Y skjedde'.

Key terms

  • gjøre det lett og rett (lett og rett) Golden Path
    Moe's load-bearing slogan — make the right action the easy action. Operationally: build tooling, frameworks, shared services, golden paths, application generators, ADR templates, hiring filters, fagtid cadence and åremål structures so that doing the right thing (secure, compliant, well-tested, well-designed) is also the path of least resistance for the practitioner. It is simultaneously a craft principle (good tools so the team can move fast safely), a leadership theory (lead through engineered defaults rather than through exhortation), and a Lean inheritance (poka-yoke; make the right way the easy way). The episode's wry title 'det eneste Vidar vil er å ha det lett' is the hosts' inversion of this: Moe wants the work to be easy precisely because he has done two decades of hard work to remove friction.
  • ha det lett
    The hosts' ironic compression of Moe's slogan into the title. Read straight it sounds like laziness ('the only thing Vidar wants is to have it easy'); read with the rest of the conversation it is the opposite — a senior practitioner whose entire career has been about engineering the conditions in which others (and he himself) can have it easy because the friction has been engineered out. The irony is generative: Moe explicitly resists titles, formal leadership, and being-not-operative — he wants to keep his hands in the work, which only works if the work is structured so that he can.
  • fagtid Craft Community
    Fixed protected craft time, every Thursday, across all fagmiljøer (professional communities). Two to eight hours depending on community. Introduced together with the faglederrolle and fagavdeling in response to the 2017–18 attrition crisis. An arena both for deep work within a discipline and for cross-pollination because everyone has fagtid simultaneously.
  • åremål (åremålsrolle)
    Fixed-term leadership role — Moe and Stian agreed each would take the faglederrolle for one year if no-one else stepped forward, on a rolling basis. The point: lower the activation energy of trying leadership; make the leadership role itself an arena to test fit; preserve the option of returning to operative work. Has continued for years and is something the org is 'veldig glad for'.
  • distribuert personalledelse
    Personnel management distributed across senior developers (rather than concentrated in dedicated managers), capped at five reports each. A Toyota-flavoured arrangement that let leaders remain operative; now being rolled back in favour of a more traditional section-leader model. Moe presents the rollback without defensiveness — the system was tuned to a moment and is being re-tuned for the next moment.
  • tech-møtet Craft Community
    The cross-team forum where shared technical decisions get made — max 50 minutes, around 25 participants, everyone physically present speaks. If a topic needs more than five minutes, it spins off into a workshop with the people who care most. The arena where new Postgres versions, security issues, and language adoptions (Java→Kotlin) are negotiated; the forum that 'in practice decides'. Escalation paths exist (IT-direktør, larger arkitektur-forum) but are rarely needed because the format is engineered for productive flat-ish discussion.
  • ADR (Architecture Decision Record) Architecture Decision Records
    Used as the working tool for explaining hard architectural choices — e.g. why the mobile bank is hybrid-native rather than pure native. Treated as the right instrument when the decision is non-intuitive and requires going 'grundig til verks' (thoroughly into the matter).
  • organisatorisk gjeld Organizational Debt
    Organisational debt — Moe coins it as 'broren til teknisk gjeld' (the brother of technical debt). His one regret-of-omission for the decade: the org could have invested more in clearing organisational debt the way it clears technical debt, especially in standardising and automating non-code parts of product development.
  • golden path Golden Path
    Used in passing to describe what he wishes existed for product development as such — a paved road with good practices baked in so that following it is easy and getting it right is automatic. Moe uses it specifically in the context of imagining roadmap-building tools with good practices encoded.
  • Toyota Way / Lean Lean & Toyota Way
    The intellectual substrate. Moe brought the physical book to the episode; calls it 'instrumentell i alt som har foregått' — instrumental in everything that has happened. Lean is the source for: kaizen / continuous improvement; A3 root-cause analysis (used to diagnose the 2017–18 attrition crisis); standardisation as the foundation for getting more from less; and arguably for the 'easy and right' principle itself (poka-yoke).
  • vitenskapelig metode (i systemutvikling) Lean & Toyota Way
    Scientific method applied to software development — Moe argues software development IS structured learning, and the best known method for structured learning is the scientific method: hypothesise (often in the form of a test), implement, verify in production, with risk minimised by making the change as small as possible. He explicitly draws the red thread from Toyota through agile to continuous delivery to scientific method, and calls this 'state of the art systemutvikling'.
  • parprogrammering Pair & Mob Programming
    Pair programming — Moe presents it both as the most effective form of knowledge sharing and as the dimension that supports quality and risk reduction. He explicitly says SpareBank 1 is not yet at full pair programming across all teams and that change in this direction is 'ordentlig seig' (truly tough/slow).
  • robuste team Pair & Mob Programming
    Robust teams — the indirect outcome of pair programming: when more than one person knows what is happening, a sick day or an absence does not stall the work.
  • endringsdyktig (carried over from his essay) Changeability & Optionality
    Carried over from his 'KI, utvikling og følelser' essay — change-capable code as the engineering goal. Not used verbatim in this episode but implied throughout: the framework team and tooling stance exist precisely so that the code (and the organisation) remain endringsdyktig.

Arguments

An engineering leader's highest-leverage move is to build the tooling, structures and arenas that make doing the right thing the easy thing — 'gjøre det lett og rett' — because engineered defaults scale further and more durably than exhortation, slides, or charisma. strength: high
Explicit premises:
  • Leading through tooling (security defaults, framework patterns, application generators) automates the right action into the day-to-day path.
  • Leading through culture and values platforms scales further than direct communication.
  • Standardising on shared tooling creates fellesskap and mobility as side effects.
  • Organisational structures (fagtid, åremål, distribuert personalledelse, tech-møtet, ADRs) can themselves be designed to make the right behaviour the easy behaviour.
Implicit premises:
  • An organisation's behaviour is more a function of its defaults than of its declared intentions.
  • Engineers will follow the path of least resistance; the lever is to engineer the path, not to harangue the engineer.
  • Tools and structures embed knowledge and value, so building them is itself a form of leadership communication.
  • The Toyota/Lean tradition (poka-yoke; standardise to free attention; continuous improvement) is the right substrate for thinking about all of the above.
Inferences:
  • Therefore, time spent building meta-tools and decision arenas is leadership work, not infrastructure work.
  • Therefore, the apparent paradox of Moe's title ('the only thing he wants is to have it easy') resolves: the work is to engineer ease, and ease earned by engineering it is not the same as laziness.
Software development is best understood as structured learning, which means scientific method (hypothesis → small change → verify in production), pair programming, continuous delivery and TDD are not techniques among many but the substrate of competent practice. strength: medium
Explicit premises:
  • Developing new programs is a learning activity.
  • Scientific method is the best known approach to structured learning.
  • Smaller changes have lower risk and faster verification, so they accelerate learning.
  • Pair programming is the most effective form of knowledge sharing.
Implicit premises:
  • There is a single, identifiable best practice for this kind of work, and it descends from the Toyota → agile → continuous-delivery lineage.
  • The organisational infrastructure (fagtid, framework team, tooling) exists to make this substrate the path of least resistance — so this argument is itself an instance of the 'lett og rett' argument.
Inferences:
  • Therefore, the way to 'do agile at scale' is not to adopt SAFe or similar but to build fagtid + hiring filters + frameworks over years.
  • Therefore, AI agent practice should be designed to preserve this substrate, not bypass it — which is the explicit thesis of Moe's prior essay 'KI, utvikling og følelser'.
Hierarchy in engineering organisations is unavoidable above ~5 people; the productive response is to design social arenas (tech-møtet, ADR practice, åremål, escalation paths) such that the inevitable hierarchy is competence-based, low-stakes-to-try, and productive of decisions rather than blocked by them. strength: high
Explicit premises:
  • Even with the same title, informal hierarchy by competence emerges in groups of 25.
  • A decision arena with a hard time limit (50 min), small enough to let everyone speak but large enough to be representative, plus workshop spin-offs, produces decisions without blocking.
  • Escalation paths to a larger architecture forum or to the IT-direktør exist but are rarely used.
  • Fixed-term leadership rotation lets people try leadership without career-cost.
Implicit premises:
  • Trying to deny or eliminate hierarchy is more costly than designing it well.
  • ADRs work as escalation tools because they force the substantive case to be made in writing rather than carried by status.
  • The point of designing the social structures is to keep the decisions close to the people doing the work.
Inferences:
  • Therefore, the right question for an engineering org is not 'flat or hierarchical?' but 'what arenas and rotations make our hierarchy productive?'
  • Therefore, the apparently boring infrastructure (a 50-minute meeting with a workshop spin-off pattern) is in fact load-bearing leadership architecture.

Narratives

  • Moe arrives at SpareBank 1 around 2004 for a code analysis of A and B business layer, then is part of his third nettbank build there as a Capgemini consultant; eventually does a short stint at Oracle while still serving SpareBank 1 as main customer; in 2012 the bank decides to start insourcing and Moe 'switches card colour' from consultant to employee, becoming one of the first.
    → Establishes the depth of context — Moe was a 'mature customer' of the bank before becoming an employee, and the insourcing moment is the founding act for everything that follows. It also makes legible his unusual stance: a consultant by background who chose to stop being a consultant, and who therefore brings a consultant's design instinct to internal engineering culture.
  • The crisis that started everything: SpareBank 1 could no longer deliver more software per unit time on a backbone (BEA WebLogic) that was being deprecated and could no longer be updated — 'a perfect storm' in 2012. Moe and Stian Conradsen (employee developer #1) build a proof of concept around 'samarbeid med web-applikasjoner', set up a strangler-pattern reverse manoeuvre, and over a couple of years restructure team boundaries around the verticals being extracted from the old monolith (cards, payments).
    → The founding crisis-and-response narrative. Lean enters here (Toyota Way physical book brought to the recording); the 'gjøre det lett og rett' instinct is articulated first as a framework-team design principle ('det skulle være så lett som mulig å kunne trekke ut en ny vertikal'); the application generators are built. This is the genesis instance from which all later instances of 'lett og rett' descend.
  • The 2017–18 attrition crisis: nearly 25% of permanent developers leave in six months, dropping headcount from 25–30 down to 18–23. The team runs an A3 (Toyota problem-solving method) and finds two root causes: missing professional development and missing belonging. Response: fagavdelinger, fagtid every Thursday, and a faglederrolle on åremål because nobody wanted the formal leader role — Moe and Stian agree to take it in alternating years if no-one else steps forward.
    → The second canonical instance of 'lett og rett' — now at the organisational layer. It also produces the åremål design, which becomes a recurring positive structure ('det å ha muligheten til å tilby noen til å prøve utledelse, det setter vi pris på'). It is the deepest evidence that Moe treats organisational design as a continuous-improvement loop.
  • Villa Paradiso at Grünerløkka, several times a year for many years — Moe meets a colleague (heavily implied from NAV: Audun's organisational world; the slogan's traceable joint provenance) over pizza and discusses fagting. The 'gjør det lett, gjør det rett' coinage emerges co-evolutionarily between the two organisations there.
    → Origin myth of the slogan itself. Both speakers want it on the record that the phrase has a specific cross-organisational provenance — not 'Moe's coinage' nor 'NAV's coinage' but a joint coinage from a pizza place. The story is itself an instance of the phenomenon Moe values: ideas that emerge in informal craft arenas and then travel into both institutions.
  • The Java → Kotlin transition: leadership says 'everyone uses Java; if you want to use Kotlin, fine — Kotlin has nice properties.' Over time the org reaches ~90/10 Kotlin organically. When asked who decided, Moe points to the tech-møtet as the de-facto decision arena. Asked what happens at 12–12, he describes the explicit escalation path: IT-direktør and a larger arkitektur-forum with information architects and security architects.
    → Worked example of the social-arena argument. The transition is presented as an instance where 'gjøre det lett og rett' worked without top-down decree: shared decision arena + permissive default + competent participants = organic 90/10 outcome without explicit policy.
  • The hybrid-native mobile-banking architecture: a case where the team had to go 'grundig til verks' multiple times to explain why the hybrid-native approach was the right choice for the org rather than a pure native approach. The argument was made in ADR form. (The hosts add that they spent a whole day with Moe arguing about what 'native' even means in that context.)
    → Worked example that 'gjøre det lett og rett' is not about avoiding hard arguments — when the right answer is non-intuitive, ADRs and patient explanation are themselves the easy-and-right tool. Also lets the hosts surface their craft kinship with Moe.
  • Audun's NAV teamkatalog example: a structured, API-exposed catalogue of teams and members that lets other support tools integrate at team granularity rather than namespace granularity — so cost and security info attaches to teams, which strengthens teams as organisational units, which strengthens the org. Moe responds 'kanskje vi skal lage noe sånn, så blir vi millionær. Det hadde vært lurt.'
    → Shows the next instance of 'gjøre det lett og rett' that Moe still wishes for: standardising and automating organisational structures (his organisatorisk gjeld point) using the same engineering instinct that already standardised the technical layer. Pre-figures the forward direction of the philosophy.
  • The closing meta-reflection: Truls notes that he kept hearing 'Toyota way' and DORA / Accelerate / Team Topologies converging — that Toyota and Lean have very clear parallels with modern DORA practice. Audun notes how striking it is that Moe always says 'we', and how hard it is to tell where the collective ends and where one person's thinking shapes it.
    → The hosts make explicit what the episode quietly enacts: the lineage that Moe operates within (Toyota → Lean → agile → DORA → Team Topologies) and the rhetorical pattern (consistent 'vi') that makes the leadership style itself an instance of engineered humility.

Questions addressed

  • ✅ How did SpareBank 1 Utvikling get from a 2012 monolithic crisis to its current vertical-team organisation?
    Through a deliberate strangler-pattern restructuring driven by a framework team that built the meta-tools (application generators, scaffolding) before pursuing velocity, with team boundaries reorganised around the verticals being extracted (cards, payments).
  • ✅ What does it actually mean to make 'easy and right' coincide?
    It means engineering the defaults — at the code layer (frameworks, generators, shared tooling for security and compliance), the social layer (tech-møtet, fagavdelinger, ADRs), and the career layer (åremål for leadership, distribuert personalledelse). Leadership through engineered defaults rather than through exhortation.
  • ✅ Why did Moe ever accept a formal leadership role?
    Because the åremål structure (fixed-term, one year, with an explicit handover agreement with Stian) made it low-stakes to try; because it was preserved as operatively compatible (capped reports, outsourced economy and recruitment); and because nobody else wanted it and there was an institutional need.
  • ✅ How do you scale alignment in a 130-developer organisation with high autonomy?
    Three layers: (1) good old-fashioned quarterly OKRs; (2) a values platform ('bedre sammen') and culture work that scales further than direct communication; (3) leadership through automation of the right defaults so the desired behaviours are the path of least resistance.
  • ✅ How do you make decisions across teams without flattening or rigidifying?
    Tech-møtet (50 min max, ~25 participants, everyone speaks) with workshop spin-offs when a topic exceeds five minutes; ADRs for non-intuitive choices; an arkitektur-forum and IT-direktør as rarely-used escalation paths. Worked example: Java→Kotlin reached 90/10 organically.
  • ✅ What is the unit of analysis for software development as such?
    Structured learning, with the scientific method as the best known instrument. Hypothesise as a test, implement, verify in production, in the smallest changes possible to minimise risk. Pair programming amplifies the learning rate.
  • ✅ What would Moe have done differently across the 14 years?
    More automation and standardisation beyond pure code — paying down 'organisatorisk gjeld' the way the org pays down technical debt. Codifying product development as a process with engineered golden paths and good practices baked into roadmap tooling. He gestures at this but does not specify the shape.
  • ⭕ What is the relationship between Moe's craft / scientific-method stance and the dominant agentic-AI development movement?
    Not explicitly addressed in this episode — pair programming, fagtid and scientific method are presented as the substrate but the agent question is deferred. (The same author's 'KI, utvikling og følelser' essay answers it; this episode is the philosophical prelude that the essay presupposes.)
  • ⭕ Can engineered defaults / golden paths produce organisational sameness or block legitimate dissent?
    The hosts circle the question (how do you handle a team that wants to do something different?), and Moe describes the tech-møtet escalation path, but the deeper question of whether the framework-team's 'right' becomes a hidden organisational orthodoxy is not engaged.
