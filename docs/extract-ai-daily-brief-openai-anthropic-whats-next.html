What OpenAI and Anthropic Think Happens Next With AI

The leading AI labs and the US federal government are entering an active negotiation over the political settlement of AI — equity stakes, governance frameworks, and pause discourse are three faces of the same bargaining process, premised on a shared (but largely unargued) belief that recursive self-improvement is already underway.

Key terms

  • Recursive self-improvement (RSI) Recursive Self-Improvement
    AI systems that build their own successors — used here as a present or near-present reality, not a sci-fi endpoint; both labs' papers are described as starting from this premise
  • Voluntarily ceding shares
    Labenz uses this in scare quotes — the labs would 'voluntarily' transfer equity to the federal government, with unclear consideration; the word voluntary is doing political work
  • AI dividend AI Regulation & Governance (External)
    Returns from government-held lab equity redistributed to American households — Altman's framing for how AI's economic benefits get broadly shared
  • Reverse federalism AI Regulation & Governance (External)
    OpenAI's proposal to scale up the best state AI regulations into federal policy rather than preempting state law
  • Dr. Frankenstein Theory AI Alignment & Safety
    Bill Gurley's frame (via All In): Anthropic believes it is not writing software but 'midwifing a deity' that will then distribute resources to humans based on AI judgment
  • Amdahl's Law (applied) Code Was Never the Bottleneck
    Speeding up one part of a process just shifts the bottleneck; here, AI cheapens ideas so organizational capacity becomes the binding constraint
  • CAISI AI Regulation & Governance (External)
    Center for AI Standards and Innovation — OpenAI wants it to remain a civilian, non-classified body that runs safety testing, not a de facto licensing regime via NSA-routed testing as in the recent EO

Arguments

The simultaneous OpenAI and Anthropic policy papers are best read as the labs' opening positions in a negotiation with government, not as safety statements addressed to each other. strength: high
Explicit premises:
  • Both papers start from the same RSI premise
  • Neither lab engages the other's arguments — both address governments, regulators, and the public
  • OpenAI's paper is a direct response to the recent White House EO
  • Anthropic commits to convening policymakers, researchers, and civil society
Implicit premises:
  • When two competitors release coordinated policy positions in the same week, they are bidding into the same institutional process
  • Policy papers from for-profit labs are strategic artifacts, not neutral analysis
  • The audience Labenz is briefing (senior tech leaders) needs to read these as negotiation moves to interpret them correctly
Inferences:
  • If the labs are negotiating, then the natural question is what they are conceding and what they are extracting — neither paper says
  • The NOTUS equity-stake story is what the labs may be implicitly trading governance frameworks against
Anthropic's pause language is at minimum strategically convenient, regardless of sincerity. strength: medium
Explicit premises:
  • Anthropic says a slowdown would be good
  • Anthropic refuses to slow unilaterally
  • The call landed right after Anthropic filed its S-1
  • Anthropic continues building the very capability (RSI) it warns about
Implicit premises:
  • Calls for industry-wide pause that one cannot honor unilaterally function as competitive constraint requests on rivals
  • An IPO-stage company asking for regulation of frontier development is asking for incumbency protection
Inferences:
  • Even if Anthropic's safety beliefs are sincere, the material effect of their position is moat-building
  • Sincerity and strategic advantage are not mutually exclusive
Equity stakes, not taxation, are the mechanism Altman prefers because they bind the government's interests to lab survival. strength: high
Explicit premises:
  • Altman pitched equity to the president directly
  • Bernie Sanders and Steve Bannon converge on a 50% tax instead
  • Critics from center and right say taxation is the correct mechanism
  • It is unclear whether the government would pay for the shares
Implicit premises:
  • A government with equity in a lab has an interest in the lab continuing to exist and succeed
  • A government that taxes a lab has an interest in extraction, which is compatible with breaking the lab up or constraining it
  • Altman is choosing the structure that makes the state a stakeholder rather than an adversary
Inferences:
  • If equity is adopted, the federal government becomes structurally aligned with the incumbent labs and against new entrants
  • The 'AI dividend' framing is the public-facing cover for what is functionally a regulatory-capture instrument
Organizational capacity, not intelligence, will be the binding constraint of the next phase. strength: high
Explicit premises:
  • Anthropic's scenario two (100-person companies doing the work of 100,000) is judged most likely
  • Amdahl's Law: speeding one part shifts the bottleneck
  • Aaron Levie: AI lowers idea cost dramatically; the binding constraint becomes execution capacity
Implicit premises:
  • Idea generation and execution capacity are separable
  • Existing organizational forms (and the humans inside them) cannot scale alongside AI-accelerated ideation
Inferences:
  • The leadership work shifts from 'find the right idea' to 'pick which AI-generated ideas to pursue and build organizations capable of executing them'
  • Research taste and judgment become the scarce, premium human skill
