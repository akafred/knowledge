Kaggle is making AI benchmark creation effortless

Andrew Wang (Google) · rss · 2026-06-04 Reader 🖥️

Agent SkillsEvals
By moving Kaggle Benchmarks into developers' local environments and letting AI coding agents author tasks in natural language, Google can scale community-built evaluations to the point where any measurable capability becomes a target that frontier labs will race to improve.

practical · expository — Product announcement framed as a call to action: developers should use Kaggle Benchmarks (now locally, now via AI coding agents) to create evaluation tasks. The expository sections explain the tooling; the closing section argues for why this matters.

Key terms

  • Benchmark / evaluation task Evals
    A discrete, scorable challenge (often a single prompt-and-expected-judgment, e.g. 'is 300+140=460 correct?') submitted to Kaggle and run against models on a public leaderboard.
  • Community-driven evaluation Evals
    Evaluations authored by the broad developer community via Kaggle's tooling, rather than by lab researchers or third-party evaluation organizations.
  • write-kaggle-benchmarks skill Agent Skills
    A Claude/agent skill (instructions + SDK reference) that teaches a coding agent how to translate a natural-language eval description into a runnable Kaggle task.
  • Trustworthy, transparent
    Public leaderboards with reproducible task definitions; the trust is in openness, not in curatorial gatekeeping.

Arguments

Google should make benchmark authoring effortless and local.
Explicit premises:
  • Reasoning agents need richer, dynamic evaluations than the classic suites provide.
  • Real-world practitioners know best what matters.
  • Until now, authoring meant working in Kaggle's web notebook — friction that suppressed participation.
Implicit premises:
  • Volume of community-authored tasks is a good proxy for evaluation quality.
  • Coding agents in IDEs are the right authoring substrate (not researchers, not domain experts in non-coding tools).
  • Goodhart effects from a 10,000+-task leaderboard ecosystem are manageable or beneficial.
Inferences:
  • The center of gravity for eval-creation shifts from research labs and academic consortia to the Kaggle platform — and therefore to Google.
  • Whoever owns the eval substrate shapes which capabilities frontier labs optimize for.
Democratizing benchmark creation benefits humanity. strength: low
Explicit premises:
  • Evaluations must reflect the full diversity of real-world challenges.
  • If a capability can be measured, labs will improve it.
Implicit premises:
  • Measured capabilities map cleanly to capabilities humanity wants improved.
  • Lab optimization is the bottleneck on AI benefiting humanity (not deployment, alignment, governance, distribution).
  • Goodhart's law — that measures become targets and cease to be good measures — does not apply at scale here.
Inferences:
  • There is no acknowledged downside to a flood of new benchmarks; the failure mode is not enumerated.

Narratives

  • The example task: an agent asked to build a benchmark that tests whether the model can judge '300+140=460 is correct?' — a featured math-false-statement task on Kaggle.
    → Shows what 'effortless' means in practice — and implicitly defines the floor of what counts as a benchmark task. Suggests trivially-scorable, single-shot judgments are the unit of measurement.
  • The '10,000+ tasks since launch' figure as a success metric.
    → Implicitly argues that volume is the right success metric for an eval marketplace — and that growth in task count is itself progress.

Questions addressed

  • ✅ How do we evaluate reasoning agents when traditional benchmarks are saturated or inadequate?
    Let the community author dynamic, real-world-grounded tasks via Kaggle, now from local IDEs with agent assistance.
  • ✅ Who is qualified to author a benchmark task?
    Anyone with an IDE and a coding agent.
  • ⭕ How do we prevent gaming, contamination, or Goodhart drift in a leaderboard of 10,000+ community-authored tasks?
  • ⭕ What is the quality-control mechanism for tasks submitted by AI agents writing tasks for AI agents?
  • ⭕ Does running on a Google-owned eval platform create a conflict of interest when Gemini models are on the same leaderboards?

Gaps

  • incompleteNo discussion of Goodhart's law — that turning a measure into a target erodes its value as a measure. With 10,000+ vendor-friendly tasks and labs explicitly racing to improve scores, this is the central risk the piece sidesteps.
    Read next: Manheim & Garrabrant on Goodhart taxonomies; recent evaluation-contamination work (Sainz et al., Zhou et al.); Gary Marcus on benchmark-overfitting in LLM math performance.
  • incompleteNo quality-control story: who reviews tasks, who detects duplicates or trivially-gameable items, who flags contamination when the same agents author both tasks and solutions.
    Read next: HELM and BIG-bench review processes; METR/Apollo on adversarial eval design; the DORA 2025 finding that AI productivity gains correlate with deteriorating evaluation rigor.
  • incompleteConflict-of-interest framing is absent. Kaggle is Google; Gemini is Google; leaderboards influence purchasing and research narrative. A genuinely 'trustworthy' eval substrate needs a story for vendor neutrality.
    Read next: MLCommons governance model; Stanford HELM independence claims; AI Snake Oil on platform-vendor evaluation.
  • incompleteThe implicit definition of 'benchmark' collapses to 'any scorable single-shot prompt.' This may be category-confusion with what the eval community means by 'benchmark' (a curated, statistically powered suite).
    Read next: Liang et al. on HELM construct validity; recent agentic-eval work (SWE-bench Verified, GAIA) on what makes a task suite vs. a task-bag.
  • uninformedNo engagement with the mounting literature showing LLM math benchmarks are heavily contaminated and that pass-rates do not transfer — the very example task ('300+140=460') is in the genre most criticized.
    Read next: Marcus & Davis on LLM arithmetic; Apple's GSM-Symbolic paper; arithmetic reasoning critiques 2024-2026.
