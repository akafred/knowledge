The secret project to settle controversial maths proof with a computer

New Scientist · rss · 2026-04 Reader 🖥️

Two independent projects are now attempting to formalise Shinichi Mochizuki's contested proof of the ABC conjecture using the Lean proof assistant, offering the first real hope of resolution since the controversy began in 2012 -- but the secret project (LANA) has already hit a roadblock at the exact point critics originally flagged.

theoretical · science journalism / narrative report — A news feature reporting on two concurrent formalisation efforts aimed at resolving the ABC conjecture proof controversy. The article is expository, not itself a mathematical argument.

Key terms

  • ABC conjecture
    A number-theory conjecture about the relationship between prime factors of integers a, b, c satisfying a + b = c
  • Inter-universal Teichmuller theory (IUT)
    The novel mathematical framework Mochizuki invented to prove ABC; the source of the controversy because it is difficult to understand
  • Formalisation (in Lean)
    Translating a written mathematical proof into machine-checkable code so its correctness can be verified automatically
  • LANA project
    Lean and Anabelian geometry project, a secret initiative led by Kato Fumiharu to formalise Mochizuki's proof and settle the controversy
  • Blueprint
    A structured plan for formalisation, breaking the proof into stages that can be incrementally verified

Arguments

Lean formalisation can resolve the ABC controversy where human dialogue could not. strength: medium
Explicit premises:
  • Human attempts to clarify the proof (2012-2018) reached an impasse.
  • Lean produces a machine-checkable record immune to misinterpretation.
  • Both Mochizuki's camp and external mathematicians are now engaged with Lean.
Implicit premises:
  • A machine-verified proof would be accepted as definitive by both sides of the dispute.
  • The disputed step in IUT is expressible in Lean at all (i.e., formalisation is technically feasible for IUT's novel constructions).
Inferences:
  • If the proof can be formalised, correctness is settled; if formalisation gets stuck at the disputed point, that itself is informative.
The fact that LANA got stuck at the Scholze-Stix point strengthens the case that the proof has a genuine gap there. strength: medium
Explicit premises:
  • LANA independently arrived at the same problematic point that Scholze and Stix flagged.
  • LANA spent 1.5 years and multiple workshops trying to resolve it without success.
Implicit premises:
  • Independent convergence on the same obstacle is evidence that the obstacle is in the proof, not in the readers.
  • If the argument were merely hard to understand (not flawed), sustained expert effort should eventually clarify it.
Inferences:
  • The disputed passage is more likely to contain a real error than a mere communication difficulty.

Questions addressed

  • ⭕ Can the ABC conjecture proof controversy be resolved?
    Two Lean formalisation projects are under way, offering the best prospect yet, but no resolution has been reached.
  • ⭕ Is Mochizuki's proof of the ABC conjecture correct?
    Still unknown. The LANA project's roadblock at the Scholze-Stix point is suggestive but not definitive.
  • ✅ Why has nobody been able to verify or refute the proof in over a decade?
    IUT introduced entirely novel concepts and techniques; human attempts at clarification reached impasse; social and institutional dynamics (Kyoto vs. rest of community) compounded the difficulty.
  • ⭕ What role can formal verification play in mathematical disputes?
    The article implicitly argues it is the only viable arbiter, but this is demonstrated only if formalisation succeeds.
