Arguments

Leaders should adopt the 'supporting character' posture rather than 'founder mode' or 'main-character energy.'
Explicit premises:
  • 'Founder mode' and 'main-character energy' push leaders toward self-centered, top-down styles.
  • Research shows these styles are deeply counterproductive.
Implicit premises:
  • Leaders choosing a narrative role (main vs. supporting character) is a meaningful and actionable frame, not merely metaphorical.
  • The alternative to self-centered leadership is team-centered leadership, and 'supporting character' accurately captures that alternative.
  • Research findings about leadership effectiveness should override culturally viral leadership ideologies.
  • What works in early-stage startups (Graham's context) does not generalize to leadership broadly.
Inferences:
  • If self-centered leadership is counterproductive, then the massive cultural influence of 'founder mode' has likely caused measurable organizational harm at scale.
  • The popularity of 'founder mode' may reveal a preference gap: leaders prefer styles that feel powerful over styles that are effective.
