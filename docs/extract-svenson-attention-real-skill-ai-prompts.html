The real skill behind good AI prompts is older than computers

Ernie Svenson · email · 2026-06-02 source 🖥️

Attention as a Hijackable ResourceContext EngineeringMeta-Skills & Contextual JudgmentPrompting as Communication
Good AI output depends not on prompting technique but on attention — the pre-digital, self-cultivated skill of noticing what matters in the task and in the model's output — which cannot be taught, only chosen.

practical · argumentative — A persuasive advice essay to professionals (framed for lawyers) about how to get better with AI. It reads partly theoretical — it explains the nature of 'attention' — but its payload is practical: change how you engage, choose to pay attention. The theoretical reframe ('context is downstream of attention') is in service of the practical exhortation.

Key terms

  • attention / noticing Prompting as Communication
    Sustained, self-directed conscious observation of the task, of oneself, and of what the AI just produced — deliberately directing your focus rather than having it captured. Ernie treats 'noticing' as its active exercise.
  • context (the AI gospel sense) Context Engineering
    The background you feed a model — accepted as real and useful, but demoted to a downstream PRODUCT of attention rather than a primary skill
  • prompting skill Prompting as Communication
    Templates, prompt libraries, 'collecting the most prompts' — explicitly deflated as NOT where the value lives
  • nurtured, not taught
    A skill developed on one's own, over time, through curiosity — no class, syllabus, or certification can transmit it

Arguments

To get better with AI, cultivate attention (choose to notice) rather than collecting prompts or chasing tools.
Explicit premises:
  • Good context requires knowing what matters about the task — what to include and leave out
  • That knowing comes from paying close attention to the work, yourself, and the AI's output
  • The fastest-improving lawyers are the attentive ones, not the prompt-collectors
  • Attention isn't teachable in a class; it's self-developed through curiosity
Implicit premises:
  • The quality of AI output is bounded mainly by the human's attention, not by the model's capability or the prompt's form
  • 'What matters about the task' is reliably knowable by attentive noticing — the relevant signal is available to be noticed
  • Attention practiced on AI work transfers and compounds rather than staying task-specific
  • The reader has the volitional capacity to redirect attention if they simply choose to
Inferences:
  • If the bottleneck is attention and attention can't be taught, then much of the prompt-course / AI-training industry is selling the wrong thing — a market critique Ernie implies but never states
  • If attention is the real, domain-general skill, AI advantage accrues to the already-attentive and could widen the gap between attentive and scattered workers
  • If context is merely downstream of attention, then better models and longer context windows will NOT close the gap for inattentive users

Narratives

  • You can lead a horse to water but can't make it drink — and with attention you can't even lead someone to it; they have to lead themselves.
    → Supports 'nurtured, not taught' — attention is irreducibly self-originated and cannot be transmitted
  • The attentive lawyer notices what worked last time, notices when the output drifted, notices the forgotten detail and adds it next round — and pulls ahead of the fire-and-forget prompt-collector who is still mediocre a year later.
    → The load-bearing evidence for both the compounding-returns claim and the prompts-don't-matter claim
  • Notifications, ads, half-finished tabs, the next thing on the list — attention captured and managed for us.
    → Illustrates that default attention is hijacked, setting up attention-as-a-choice-you-must-reclaim

Questions addressed

  • ✅ Why do some people get far more out of AI than others?
    Because they pay attention and notice as they go, not because they have better prompts or models.
  • ✅ Can the skill behind good AI use be taught?
    No — it's nurtured through self-directed curiosity, not transmitted in a class.
  • ⭕ HOW does one actually cultivate attention?
    Only gestured at ('be curious', 'choose it', join the paid Inner Circle to 'slow down together'). The claim that attention can't be taught conveniently forecloses the how-to question the essay itself raises.
  • ⭕ Is it actually true that model capability and prompt form matter little relative to attention?
