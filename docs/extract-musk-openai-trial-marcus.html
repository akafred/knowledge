What matters (or should matter), at the Musk-OpenAI trial

Gary Marcus from Marcus on AI · email · 2026-05-05 source 🖥️

The Musk-OpenAI trial has surfaced damaging evidence — especially from Greg Brockman's diary — that OpenAI leadership deliberately deceived Musk about their commitment to the nonprofit mission, and regardless of who prevails legally, the revealed pattern of institutional deception is what should matter most to the public.

Key terms

  • Brockman's diary
    Private journal of OpenAI co-founder Greg Brockman, entered as evidence, containing entries from November 2017 revealing awareness that pushing Musk out while later converting to a for-profit structure would constitute a lie.
  • nonprofit mission / B-corp pivot
    OpenAI was founded as a nonprofit; the diary reveals Brockman acknowledged that a later pivot to a for-profit entity (B-corp) would retroactively make their representations to Musk dishonest.
  • jury trial
    The fact-finder is a lay jury, not a judge — making outcome inherently unpredictable regardless of legal merits.
