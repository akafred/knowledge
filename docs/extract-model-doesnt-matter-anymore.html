Acunzo supplies a criterion from the creative-work side: work grounded in and expressing the creator's non-reproducible firsthand lived experience and distinctive perspective is irreplaceable, whereas work that merely rearranges secondhand information ('a script the creator forgot to act') is automatable and prone to slop. It is a criterion, not an engineering method, but it directly answers the 'what makes it irreplaceable' question the other piece leaves open.
