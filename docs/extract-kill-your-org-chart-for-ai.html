The founder of Postman says you have to kill your existing org chart

Ivan Burazin · tweet · 2026-04-10 Reader 🖥️

Coordination CostHierarchy & Org ChartsAgencyNon-Engineers BuildingTaste & Judgment
Traditional organizational hierarchies must be dismantled in favor of flat, builder-centric structures where everyone — including PMs and designers — ships directly, and management exists to cultivate judgment rather than coordinate through layers.

practical · tweet (summary/commentary on a talk or interview) — This is a tweet thread summarizing and endorsing claims made by Abhinav Asthana (@a85), founder of Postman, about how AI should reshape organizational structure. Ivan Burazin is relaying and amplifying the message. The form constrains depth — these are compressed assertions, not developed arguments.

Key terms

  • org chart Hierarchy & Org Charts
    The formal hierarchy of reporting relationships and layers of management that mediate between leadership and individual contributors
  • wide span of control Hierarchy & Org Charts
    Each manager (including executives) oversees many direct reports, minimizing intermediate layers
  • high agency Agency
    The capacity and authority to act autonomously, see broadly across the organization, and drive projects end-to-end without waiting for permission or coordination through management layers
  • building
    Directly producing artifacts (code, prototypes, APIs, designs that ship) as opposed to producing documents, specs, or coordination overhead
  • judgment Taste & Judgment
    The ability to make good decisions about what to build, prioritize, and ship — positioned as management's redefined purpose

  • explicitThe modern org chart requires wide span of control, direct work with ICs, and a binary of building or selling.conf: high

Arguments

Organizations must flatten their hierarchies and make everyone a builder. strength: medium
Explicit premises:
  • AI tools enable non-engineers to ship production code and prototypes directly.
  • The alternative to building is selling — there is no third category of pure coordination.
  • Staff/principal engineers with high agency can lead projects without management intermediaries.
Implicit premises:
  • The primary function of middle management layers was coordination and translation between executives and ICs.
  • AI tools are sufficiently mature and reliable for PMs and designers to ship production-quality work.
  • What Postman has done internally is generalizable to other organizations.
  • Speed and directness of execution are the dominant competitive advantages in the AI era.
Inferences:
  • If everyone can build directly, then coordination layers lose their justification.
  • If coordination layers are removed, management must find a new purpose (judgment).

Narratives

  • The transformation at Postman: PMs who used to write PRDs now prototype in Claude; designers who used to hand off Figma files now ship PRs through Cursor.
    → Provides concrete, aspirational examples of role transformation to make the abstract org-chart argument tangible and credible

Questions addressed

  • ✅ How should organizations restructure for the AI era?
    Flatten hierarchy, widen span of control, eliminate layers between execs and ICs, make everyone a builder, redefine management as judgment-development.
  • ✅ What happens to PMs and designers in an AI-native org?
    They become builders — shipping code, APIs, and PRs directly using AI tools.
  • ⭕ What is management for if not coordination?
    Developing better judgment — but this is asserted without elaboration on what that means in practice.

Gaps

  • unstated assumptionThe argument assumes that coordination, alignment, and context-sharing across teams are either unnecessary or automatically handled in flat structures. Large-scale organizations face coordination problems that are not solely caused by hierarchy but by the inherent complexity of distributed work.
    Read next: Literature on coordination costs in flat organizations (e.g., Galbraith's information-processing theory of organization design); case studies of flat-org failures at scale.
  • missing evidenceNo evidence is provided that PMs building APIs or designers shipping PRs produces better outcomes than the prior division of labor. The implicit claim is that directness equals quality, but this conflates shipping speed with product quality.
    Read next: Research on specialization vs. generalization in knowledge work; outcomes data from companies that have adopted these structures.
  • ambiguity'Management's job is to develop better judgment' is left undefined. Judgment about what? How is it developed? This is the most provocative redefinition in the thread but receives the least elaboration.
    Read next: Explore what 'judgment' means operationally — cf. Kahneman on decision quality, or strategic management literature on executive cognition.
  • selection biasThe argument is attributed to the founder of Postman — a developer-tools company where engineering culture is dominant. The prescription may not transfer to organizations where the core work is not software (healthcare, logistics, manufacturing).
    Read next: Compare with AI-era org design thinking in non-tech industries.
  • unacknowledged tensionThe binary 'either you're building, or you're selling' erases important organizational functions: strategy, research, compliance, people development, customer success. These are neither building nor selling but may be essential.
    Read next: Consider which organizational functions are genuinely automatable vs. which are being rhetorically dismissed.
