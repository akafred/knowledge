LLMs Corrupt Your Documents When You Delegate

Philippe Laban, Tobias Schnabel, Jennifer Neville · article · 2026-04-17 source ↗ Reader 🖥️

EvalsHuman-in-the-LoopLLM LimitationsVibe Coding
Current LLMs are unreliable delegates that silently corrupt documents during long delegated workflows, as demonstrated by the DELEGATE-52 benchmark across 52 professional domains and 19 models.

practical · argumentative — Empirical benchmark study with a clear argumentative thrust: current LLMs are unreliable delegates. Practical because it directly addresses the readiness of deployed AI systems for real-world delegation workflows.

Key terms

  • delegation LLM Limitations
    An interaction paradigm where users hand off document-editing tasks to an LLM with the expectation of faithful execution — distinct from conversational or co-editing use
  • DELEGATE-52 Evals
    A benchmark simulating long delegated workflows requiring in-depth document editing across 52 professional domains
  • document corruption LLM Limitations
    Unintended degradation of document content introduced by the LLM during editing, measured as percentage of content corrupted
  • vibe coding Vibe Coding
    Used as an example of delegated work — coding driven by high-level intent rather than precise specification

Arguments

Current LLMs are unreliable delegates for document editing workflows. strength: high
Explicit premises:
  • DELEGATE-52 simulates long delegated workflows across 52 professional domains
  • 19 LLMs were tested on this benchmark
  • Even frontier models corrupt ~25% of document content by the end of long workflows
  • Non-frontier models fail more severely
Implicit premises:
  • 25% content corruption is an unacceptable error rate for professional document editing
  • The 52 domains in DELEGATE-52 are representative of real-world delegation use cases
  • The simulated workflow lengths are representative of actual delegated sessions
Inferences:
  • If the best models corrupt a quarter of content, the technology is not ready for unsupervised delegation
The delegation problem is fundamental, not an artifact of tooling limitations. strength: medium
Explicit premises:
  • Agentic tool use does not improve performance on DELEGATE-52
Implicit premises:
  • If the problem were about capability gaps (e.g., inability to read files), tool use would help
  • The tested agentic setups are representative of current tool-use approaches
Inferences:
  • The corruption stems from something deeper than missing tools — likely the models' inability to maintain document fidelity during extended editing

Questions addressed

  • ✅ Are current LLMs ready to be trusted with delegated document editing across professional domains?
    No — even frontier models corrupt ~25% of content in long workflows.
  • ✅ Does agentic tool use mitigate document corruption in delegated workflows?
    No — agentic tool use does not improve performance.
  • ✅ What factors exacerbate document degradation?
    Document size, interaction length, and presence of distractor files.
  • ⭕ What mitigations or solutions could reduce delegation-induced corruption?
    Not addressed in the abstract — the paper diagnoses the problem but does not propose fixes.
