10 uses for a pocket notebook to keep you off your phone

Amy Catriona · article · 2026-05-17 source ↗ Reader 🖥️

Analog Capture PracticeAttention as a Hijackable Resource
Carrying a small, cheap, always-available paper notebook gives you a competing thing to reach for in idle moments, which displaces habitual phone-scrolling and lets you spend those reclaimed moments on something more meaningful.

practical · mixed (narrative frame + expository listicle) — Personal-essay hook establishing a problem (compulsive phone-scrolling), followed by a ten-item how-to list; light theoretical claim (substitution beats willpower) is implicit rather than argued.

Key terms

  • pocket notebook Analog Capture Practice
    A specific small, low-cost, always-carried analog notebook whose psychological role is to be an alternative habitual object to the phone, not merely a note-taking tool.
  • commonplace book Analog Capture Practice
    Explicitly restricted (per her closing footnote) to copies of external sources only — quotes, passages, lines — deliberately excluding her own thoughts or opinions, distinguishing it from a general 'catch-all' notebook.
  • worry list
    A specific therapy-taught technique: a written list of current worries, each optionally paired with a one-line reason it doesn't need attention right now.

Arguments

Keeping a pocket notebook is an effective, low-cost way to reduce habitual phone-scrolling in idle moments and redirect that time toward something more meaningful. strength: low
Explicit premises:
  • Phone-scrolling in spare/gap moments rarely felt nourishing or meaningful
  • The notebook is small, portable, cheap to keep stocked, and always available
  • It offers many distinct, flexible uses (practical, reflective, creative, silly) that can meet whatever need arises in the moment
Implicit premises:
  • Having a physical alternative object ready to hand interrupts the habitual reach for the phone — i.e., environment design beats willpower
  • The specific content written matters less than the fact that the habitual impulse gets redirected somewhere
Inferences:
  • Generalizes from a single first-person case ('it helps me') to a general recommendation, via the instructional framing 'here are ten ways I use mine' addressed to the reader

Questions addressed

  • ✅ Does keeping a pocket notebook reduce phone-scrolling in idle moments?
    Yes, for the author subjectively — she reports it as 'genuinely powerful' for reclaiming small moments in her day.
  • ✅ What can a pocket notebook be used for?
    Ten enumerated uses: reviews, commonplace book, favourites lists, pocket games, overheard conversations, new words, worry lists, distractions list, sketches/doodles, notes to self.
  • ⭕ Does this practice generalize to people who don't already enjoy writing, or who lack private/portable moments in their day?
  • ⭕ Will the practice's effectiveness persist past its current novelty, or is it liable to fade like other habit-substitution attempts?
