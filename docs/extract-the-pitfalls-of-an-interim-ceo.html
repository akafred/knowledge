Arguments

Interim CEO appointments should not be treated as a safe default response to sudden leadership gaps
Explicit premises:
  • Research shows interim appointments trigger negative stakeholder reactions
  • Research shows interim appointments lead to long-term value loss
Implicit premises:
  • The rationale for interim appointments (buying time, reducing short-term disruption) does not outweigh the empirical costs
  • Boards underestimate the signaling costs of the 'interim' label — it communicates uncertainty rather than stability to markets and employees
  • A CEO with 'interim' status faces structural constraints on authority that impair decision-making and talent retention
Inferences:
  • If interim appointments are costly but boards continue to choose them, the decision is driven by risk aversion to the search process itself — not a rational assessment of options
