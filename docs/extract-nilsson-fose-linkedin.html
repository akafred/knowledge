Reflecting on Thoughtworks' 'Future of Software Development' retreat, the author reports that leading practitioners have moved past the hype-versus-doom polarisation about agentic coding toward nuanced 'when, where and how' thinking, converging on the view that enduring engineering fundamentals (DDD, XP, TOC, TDD, BDD, CD) plus a renewed 'Extreme Humanism' matter more than ever in the agentic age — which is why he is optimistic.

Key terms

  • Extreme Humanism / conspicuously human
    A recognised need, voiced by several participants, to deliberately amplify the distinctly human dimension of software work as coding is increasingly automated
  • the fundamentals (DDD, XP, TOC, TDD, BDD, CD)
    The set of long-standing engineering disciplines the author 'always come[s] back to', held to be more important than ever in the agentic age
  • when, where and how
    The nuanced, situational framing that replaced blanket judgements about agentic coding at the retreat
  • individuals and interactions
    The human element of the gathering, which the author says was 'the thing' — an explicit echo of the first value of the Agile Manifesto

Arguments

The future of software development in the agentic age warrants optimism. strength: low
Explicit premises:
  • At the retreat the discourse on agentic coding was nuanced and balanced rather than polarised.
  • Participants held that enduring engineering fundamentals (DDD, XP, TOC, TDD, BDD, CD) will be more important than ever.
  • Several participants recognised the need for 'Extreme Humanism' / being conspicuously human.
  • The individuals and interactions — the human element — were the most valuable part of the gathering.
Implicit premises:
  • A mature, balanced discourse among leading practitioners is a good predictor of a healthy trajectory for the field.
  • Grounding in durable fundamentals plus a deliberate focus on the human dimension mitigates the risks of agentic coding.
Inferences:
  • Because the leading conversation is mature, grounded in durable fundamentals, and human-centred, the direction of travel for software development is positive.

Narratives

  • The retreat's image showed a '25' marking 25 years since the Agile Manifesto, yet the author is 'not sure I heard the word "agile" once' during the workshop.
    → Signals that agile has been fully internalised and that the frontier of attention has moved on to the agentic future rather than to methodology labels.

Questions addressed

  • ✅ Does the rise of agentic coding call for despair or uncritical enthusiasm?
    Neither — the productive stance is nuanced 'when, where and how', moving past the evil-versus-panacea polarisation.
  • ✅ Does agentic AI make traditional software-engineering fundamentals obsolete?
    No — participants reportedly agreed they are and will be more important than ever.
  • ⭕ What should anchor software work as coding is increasingly automated?
    The human element — 'Extreme Humanism', 'conspicuously human', and 'individuals and interactions'.
