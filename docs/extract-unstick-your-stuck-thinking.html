Key terms

  • Thinkies Meta-Skills & Contextual Judgment
    Learnable pattern transformations that shift how you see a situation, expand options, and spark ideas — especially when stuck or prematurely decided
  • Can't/Because
    A specific Thinkie pattern where a 'can't X because Y' statement is decomposed: accepting the causal claim provisionally to generate solutions to Y, which unsticks the conversation
  • learnable pattern transformations
    Repeatable cognitive moves that can be taught and practiced, each transforming a thinking pattern into a different one

Arguments

You should learn Thinkies (and attend TWC II) to become better at unsticking your thinking strength: low
Explicit premises:
  • Thinkies are learnable pattern transformations
  • They help you notice stuck patterns and shift perspective
  • The goal is generating many ideas, not finding the one right idea
Implicit premises:
  • Getting stuck in thinking patterns is a common and costly problem
  • Quantity of ideas reliably leads to better outcomes than searching for the single right idea
  • Pattern recognition and transformation are skills that improve with practice
Inferences:
  • The Can't/Because dialogue demonstrates rapid idea generation from a stuck position, implying the other ~99 Thinkies are similarly productive

Narratives

  • A dialogue where someone says 'We can't deploy more often because we have too many defects.' The interlocutor flips it: 'So when we have fewer defects we can deploy more often?' — then immediately generates ideas (writing tests, incident reviews). The first speaker is surprised at the rapid ideation.
    → Demonstrates the Can't/Because Thinkie in action — shows how accepting the causal link and solving for the cause unsticks the conversation and generates options
