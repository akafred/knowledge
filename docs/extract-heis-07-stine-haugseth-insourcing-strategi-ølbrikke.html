7) Stine Haugseth: Insourcing, retning og strategi på en ølbrikke

Truls Jørgensen og Audun Fauchald Strand — Heis Podcast · podcast · 2025-12-15 source 🖥️

Data SovereigntyGo to the Floor (Gemba)Insourcing & In-House CapabilityPlatform EngineeringProfessional IdentityPublic-Sector DigitalizationStrategy & Direction-SettingTeam Autonomy
Insourcing, retning, and strategi-på-en-ølbrikke are three faces of one leadership move: pulling the locus of decision-making in-house and then compressing direction tightly enough that a distributed organisation can actually act on it — insourcing repatriates the people who must own the work, oppdragsbasert ledelse and sirkler av autonomi give those people a clear mission with explicit frihetsgrenser, and the ølbrikke forces utviklingsstrategi down to the few hovedområder a development organisation can actually carry, with OKRs underneath and an explicit business case underneath that.

practical · narrative-expository — a leadership case interview built around three intertwined arcs: an autobiographical arc (NAV-analytics → forsikring → Spink revelation → Mattilsynet rebuild → SpareBank 1 Østlandet), an organisational practice arc (the operational mechanics of insourcing, oppdragsbasert ledelse, sponsor-vs-styringsgruppe, sirkler av autonomi), and a compression-as-method arc (utviklingsstrategi reduced to an ølbrikke, plus OKR underneath). Hosts ask short questions; Haugseth answers at length. Practical-leaning throughout; argumentative on insourcing's business-case requirement and the sky/skye reversal; narrative on the Mattilsynet contract cancellation window, the Spink team, and the Sporveien driftssjef. — Both hosts have shared NAV history with Haugseth ('jeg jobbet i Ønsenjongen der du jobber i dag... tror jeg nesten samme avdeling'), which surfaces in shorthand references to NAV's 2016 insourcing journey and to NAIS-as-a-service.

Key terms

  • Insourcing (hjemtaking) Insourcing & In-House Capability
    Repatriating the locus of work — and the decision-making that comes with it — by terminating consultant contracts and hiring in-house. In Haugseth's operational vocabulary, insourcing is governed by (1) a window (when can you cancel? Mattilsynet's 2-per-year cancellation gates), (2) a snowball effect ('hvis du får tak i noen skikkelig flinke folk... så vil de kunne få tak i enda flere'), (3) a competence prerequisite ('du må vite hvem du skal insource' — kvoting Torbjørn Larsen: 'du må ha peiling for å skrive på peiling. Også for å ansette peiling'), and (4) a business case ('konsulentene får ny oppdrag andre steder, ELLER vi får boostet inntektene våre').
  • Retning Strategy & Direction-Setting
    Direction-setting in a way that is intelligible at the team level — concrete enough to act on, abstract enough to leave room for execution choices. For Haugseth, retning is enacted through (a) oppdragsbeskrivelser, (b) frihetsgrenser, (c) compressed strategy artifacts like the ølbrikke, and (d) ritual repetition (the Sporveien T-bane example).
  • Strategi på en ølbrikke Strategy & Direction-Setting
    An utviklingsstrategi (not virksomhetsstrategi) compressed to fit on a beer coaster — three or so hovedområder named explicitly (eierskap, egen art, lønnsomhet in Mattilsynet's case), with more concrete strategiske føringer below and OKRs as the operationalisation layer. The ølbrikke is a method, not a metaphor: they literally printed it. Its function is to make the development-side translation of the corporate strategy short enough that everyone can carry it.
  • Oppdragsbasert ledelse Team Autonomy
    Mission-based leadership borrowed from the (Nordic) military: leaders specify mål and rammer; subordinates choose how to reach them. Haugseth contrasts it with the trinn-for-trinn (Soviet) model that collapses under contact with reality, and cites Ukraine/Russia as recent reinforcement. Operationally: a one-pager oppdragsbeskrivelse from konsernledelse to each team, with a small sponsorgruppe — NOT a styringsgruppe.
  • Sirkler av autonomi Team Autonomy
    Concentric authority structure: the team decides everything inside its circle; cross-team things are negotiated horizontally; only a few high-altitude things require top-level decision. Haugseth attributes the framing to a Netflix presentation she saw 'før vi begynte å snakke om aligned autonomy.'
  • Sponsorgruppe (ikke styringsgruppe)
    A small group (two members) from the toppledergruppe explicitly co-responsible for a team's success — sitting beside the team rather than above it. Designed to spread eierskap across konsernledelsen so that the teams stop being 'Stine sine team' and become 'våre team.'
  • Hjemtaking av jurister Insourcing & In-House Capability
    An under-emphasised aspect of Mattilsynet insourcing: jurists were brought in alongside developers so regelverksutvikling stopped being an external loop. The principle generalises: insourcing's value is partly about which inter-organisational loops it collapses.
  • Visjonær lederstil
    Her favourite style: give technologists the freeway, clear obstacles, hold the ambisjon high enough that they can run. Method (not innate): repeated articulation of 'hvorfor er vi på jobb' until everyone carries the same answer — the Sporveien driftssjef's 'vi kjører T-banen' as the archetype.
  • Hospitering Go to the Floor (Gemba)
    Sitting in teamrom, walking the gulvet, listening — her main learning loop as toppleder. Yields arbeidsglede, impulser, and exposes her to genuine teknolog-disagreements that re-shape her world model.
  • Skye / sky-strategi Data Sovereignty
    Her changed view: from full pro-AWS/GCP advocate ('bare kjører vi') to 'kanskje ikke alt skal opp i skye' — driven by beredskap-arbeidet at Mattilsynet, geopolitisk endring, and kostnadshits. The corrective: exit-strategi, beredskap, fallback-løsninger. Finanssektor is lovpålagt to have exit-strategi; she wants everyone to think about it.
  • NAIS as a service Platform Engineering
    NAV's application platform (NAIS), offered as a tjeneste to other public etater. Haugseth advocated piloting it at Mattilsynet; the decision went another way. In hindsight: she would have used it. The most valuable thing is not the plattform itself but the community / grupperingen — a way of thinking about verdiforbruker and forvaltbar kode over tid.
  • Business case for insourcing Insourcing & In-House Capability
    Insourcing must show measurable gevinst — either consultants are redeployed productively elsewhere, or rådgiver-hverdagen improves enough to boost inntekt. 'Du må vite hva du skal ha som mål med det.'
  • Privilegium, ikke identitet Professional Identity
    The toppleder-rolle is borrowed time. 'Ikke få tett og klamt eierskap til en lederrolle.' Someone else may be better for a steady-state phase later.

Arguments

Insourcing is the right operational move for software-heavy organisations — but it must be governed by a business case, a competence prerequisite, and an exploitation of the contract window. strength: high
Explicit premises:
  • Software competence pays back faster than other competence categories when brought in-house.
  • Insourcing requires the leader to know who to hire — i.e., the leader needs enough tech understanding to filter candidates.
  • Insourcing must produce a gevinst — consultants redeployed elsewhere, or productivity boosting inntekt.
  • Contract structure dictates the timing — Mattilsynet had only twice-yearly cancellation windows, which forced the move on a 3-4 month runway.
Implicit premises:
  • The leader's epistemic warrant for insourcing decisions is part of the move itself; an insourcing leader without tech literacy reproduces consultant-dependence at a new layer.
  • Insourcing's snowball effect — flinke folk attract flinke folk — makes the first hires disproportionately load-bearing.
  • Insourcing creates the conditions under which oppdragsbasert ledelse and small cross-functional teams become viable.
Inferences:
  • A leader without a business case for insourcing should not insource.
  • A leader without tech literacy should not be the one driving insourcing decisions.
  • Insourcing's value depends partly on which inter-organisational loops it collapses (e.g., bringing jurists in alongside developers).
Retning compressed to a one-pager and an ølbrikke does more for direction-coherence than heavyweight strategy documents or trinn-for-trinn instructions. strength: high
Explicit premises:
  • Trinn-for-trinn collapses under contact with reality (military example).
  • An oppdragsbeskrivelse + small sponsor group gives the team a clear mission with concrete rammer.
  • An utviklingsstrategi on an ølbrikke compresses direction to what people can carry; mer konkrete føringer and OKRs do the operationalisation below.
Implicit premises:
  • Compression is a forcing function for deselection — what doesn't fit on the ølbrikke isn't really strategi.
  • Direction held only by senior leaders is direction the gulvet cannot act on.
  • Repetition is the medium by which compressed direction becomes shared (the Sporveien example).
Inferences:
  • If your utviklingsstrategi cannot fit on a coaster, you have not yet done the strategy work.
  • If your strategy does not propagate down to OKR-level work, you have a powerpoint, not a strategi.
  • The combined oppdragsbeskrivelse + ølbrikke + OKR stack is itself a way of distributing decision-rights at different altitudes.
Sponsor groups (not styringsgrupper) and shared eierskap across konsernledelse are the institutional vehicles by which 'Stine sine team' become 'våre team' — without which transformasjons-arbeid runs as a parallel structure that the rest of the organisation ignores. strength: high
Explicit premises:
  • Smidig transformasjon easily creates a parallel struktur the rest of the organisation lets live fritt.
  • A two-person sponsorgruppe with medansvar invests konsernledelsen in the team's success.
  • Avdelingsledere/seksjonsledere must be ansvarliggjort for cross-team samhandling, not allowed to drift into administrasjon.
Implicit premises:
  • Without shared eierskap, the transformation lead becomes the only person accountable, which is unstable.
  • Ledergrupper that are not held responsible for cross-team samhandling will degenerate into status-meetings.
  • Shared eierskap is itself a leadership artefact — it requires deliberate design (the sponsor model) rather than just expectation.
Inferences:
  • If your smidig transformasjon is led by a single person's team, it is institutionally fragile.
  • If your ledergruppe meetings don't deal with cross-team samhandling, your operating model has a structural gap.
  • The right size of a 'sponsor' is small — a heavyweight styringsgruppe re-imports the parallel-structure problem.
Insourcing logic extends to the platform layer — sky-strategi is itself a question of where the locus of control should live. strength: medium
Explicit premises:
  • Geopolitical and cost dynamics now argue against full sky-avhengighet.
  • Beredskap, fallback, and exit-strategi are increasingly required.
  • Finansbransjen is lovpålagt exit-strategi; other sectors should adopt the discipline anyway.
Implicit premises:
  • The same logic that justifies insourcing software competence — keep the locus of decision-making close — applies to infrastructure.
  • A leader's view of where 'verden beveger seg' (cf. Olrud Moen ep 15) must include geopolitical and supplier-concentration trends, not only product/market trends.
  • Sky-decisions made on cost-per-compute alone underweight the cost of lock-in.
Inferences:
  • If you cannot articulate your exit-strategi from your hovedskyleverandør, you are over-leveraged.
  • If your sky-strategi has not been re-examined since 2021-2022, it may be running on premises that have shifted.
  • Sovereign / European / Nordic cloud alternatives deserve active utforskning.
The insourcing prescription has a limit — small offentlige etater should NOT replicate the plattform-bygging of the large ones; the highest-value piggyback is community, not code. strength: medium
Explicit premises:
  • NAIS at NAV represents enormous investment that smaller etater cannot replicate well.
  • The most valuable thing NAIS-as-a-service brings is not the technical platform but the surrounding community / grupperingen and its way of thinking about verdiforbruker and forvaltbar kode.
  • Mattilsynet's decision to not pilot NAIS is, in retrospect, one Haugseth would have made differently.
Implicit premises:
  • Communities of practice transfer tacit knowledge that platforms alone cannot encode.
  • The 'build vs. buy' decision underweights 'join a community' as a third option.
  • Insourcing as a frame can mislead by treating ownership-of-stack as the only locus question.
Inferences:
  • A small etat that builds its own plattform pays twice — once for the platform, again for the missing community.
  • Platform-as-a-service offerings should be evaluated partly on the strength of their surrounding community.
  • Haugseth's general insourcing pro-stance has a clear, named exception.
Lederstil should be chosen for situation, not signature — visjonær for high-ambition technologist teams, more direktiv for those who need clearer rammer, harder/stricter for handling underperformance and the 'kjipe' part of the role. strength: medium
Explicit premises:
  • Different mennesker, teams and organisations respond to different lederstiler.
  • Haugseth's favoritt is visjonær; her least favourite but necessary work is handling people 'i russ.'
  • Diagnosing which stil a situation needs is itself part of the leadership craft.
Implicit premises:
  • A signature lederstil is a refusal to do the diagnostic work the situation requires.
  • The role is borrowed — even one's preferred style should not become identity (the 'privilegium ikke identitet' frame).
  • Treating leadership as a repertoire is what makes oppdragsbasert ledelse possible — the same leader can move between altitudes.
Inferences:
  • If you only have one lederstil, you are limited to the situations it fits.
  • If you cannot do the kjipe oppgavene with the same craft as the visjonære ones, you cannot hold the role.
  • The right time to leave a role may arrive before you stop being effective in it.

Narratives

  • Mattilsynet insourcing: Haugseth arrives roughly a year after Inger Helene (predecessor's predecessor — name uncertain in transcript) was tasked with cleaning up Mattilsynet following a graverende KPMG-rapport that cost the previous administrerende the role.   • NAV insourcing 2016: started slowly compared to Mattilsynet — 1, 2, 3, 5 — before scaling. Reference point for how unusual the Mattilsynet pace was.

  • The Sporveien T-bane driftssjef: many years ago, when Haugseth was working at (Bærums)værnsnøyen / Sporveien, the operations chief in the T-bane division ran 1-1 or classroom sessions with every newcomer. The signature was a single drilled question: 'hvorfor er du på jobb?' If you answered 'jeg skal sitte og skrive litt kode,' the response was 'nei, du kjører T-banen.' The result: 'du gikk inn døra... at her er det noe annet her, som ikke du ser alle andre steder.'

  • The ølbrikke: Haugseth's team in Mattilsynet drafted an utviklingsstrategi with three hovedområder (eierskap, egen art, lønnsomhet) compact enough to fit on a beer coaster — and then literally printed it on coasters. Below the three hovedområder ran more konkrete strategiske føringer, with OKRs as the operationalisation layer.
    → The episode's titular method — strategy compressed to its essential, then layered down through concrete føringer and OKRs.
  • NAIS-as-a-service: Haugseth advocated strongly that Mattilsynet pilot NAV's plattform-as-a-service (NAIS) — there were ulike utvikler-preferences for thin-plattform vs. Golden Path. The decision went another way (Mattilsynet stayed on its tynne, krevende plattform). In hindsight she would have moved to NAIS sooner — the most valuable thing was not the platform itself but the community and grupperingen connected to it.
    → Admitted strategic miss; sharp counterexample to the 'build your own platform' instinct in smaller etater.
  • The skye-reversal: Haugseth was previously full pro-AWS/GCP ('bare kjører vi') for utviklingsverktøy and cost; now is markedly more critical. Drivers: beredskaps-arbeidet at Mattilsynet, recent geopolitisk endring, sterke prishit fra tek-leverandørene, and the question 'vil vi bruke skattepengene våre på det?' Finanssektor's lovpålagte exit-strategi is, in her view, a discipline more sectors should adopt.
    → Concrete worked example of a leader updating her view, and an extension of the insourcing logic to the platform layer.

Questions addressed

  • ✅ What does insourcing actually deliver, and why is it the right move for software-heavy organisations?
    Cost savings + tight coupling of developers to the organisation; collapses inter-organisational loops; with a snowball effect on hiring quality. But governed by a business case and a competence prerequisite — and not always full insourcing (see SpareBank 1 Østlandet).
  • ✅ When should a leader take an insourcing window, and what determines pace?
    Contract structure determines the window (Mattilsynet's twice-yearly cancellation gates); pace depends on local conditions (Mattilsynet scaled fast; NAV 2016 was much slower at the start).
  • ✅ Does insourcing extend beyond developers?
    Yes — Mattilsynet's colocation of jurists with developers eliminated a regelverks-loop; the principle generalises to any cross-organisational loop where colocation reduces coordination cost.
  • ✅ How do you set retning for a smidig organisation without micro-managing?
    Oppdragsbasert ledelse — one-pager oppdragsbeskrivelse from konsernledelse, two-person sponsorgruppe co-responsible for team success, sirkler av autonomi making explicit what is decided inside the team vs. coordinated vs. escalated.
  • ✅ How do you scale the one-pager + sponsor model?
    An intermediate produktområde layer (her NAV term) acts as the framing structure when there are too many teams for direct toppleder-presence.
  • ✅ How do you keep utviklingsstrategi from becoming nok et dokument i skuffen?
    Compress to an ølbrikke — three hovedområder readable in one glance, mer konkrete strategiske føringer below, OKRs as the operationalisation layer.
  • ✅ How does a tech leader stay grounded as the role moves them further from the gulvet?
    Hospitering — sit in teamrom, walk the gulvet, listen for technologist disagreements as the highest-leverage learning input.
  • ✅ How important is the leader's own tech literacy in insourcing-heavy organisations?
    Critical — without it you cannot recruit well or insource well; insourcing without tech literacy reproduces consultant-dependence at a new layer ('du må ha peiling for å skrive på peiling').
  • ✅ Has the case for cloud-first changed?
    Yes — geopolitisk endring, prishit, beredskap-krav all argue for fallback-løsninger and exit-strategi. Finansbransjen is lucky to be lovpålagt this discipline; other sectors should adopt it anyway.
  • ⭕ When does the insourcing instinct go wrong?
    Implicitly named in the NAIS-reflection: small offentlige etater should NOT replicate large players' plattform-bygging — they should piggyback. But the criterion for when to insource a stack vs. join a community is not generalised.
  • ⭕ What about insourcing efforts that fail outright?
    Not explored. The conversation is structured around successful insourcing (Mattilsynet, NAV) and against the build-your-own-platform instinct, but contains no narrative of an insourcing that produced bad outcomes.
  • ⭕ What do you owe the consultants who lose oppdrag when you insource?
    Only briefly named in the business-case framing ('konsulentene får ny oppdrag andre steder'). Whether that absorption actually happens, and what leaders owe the people whose contracts they terminate, is not analysed.

The closest is the NAIS-reflection (where she would have stayed less ambitious about own platform), but that is presented as 'should have piggybacked,' not 'insourcing failed.' The asymmetry shapes what is visible: insourcing's hazards (talent risk, two-year ramp where the org loses access to specialists, capability concentration in a few key hires who then leave) are absent from the framing.
