AI inference is the enduring, last-standing market because intelligence only begets more intelligence, and the companies that win it will combine software excellence with strategic control of scarce compute supply.

practical · mixed — A CEO interview mixing market analysis (theoretical) with strategic prescriptions for how companies should approach inference, custom models, and scaling (practical). The conversation is argumentative in structure — hosts probe, guest defends positions.

Key terms

  • inference cloud Compute & Energy Infrastructure
    A unified runtime fabric spanning many physical clouds, optimized specifically for serving AI model inference at scale — not just GPU-as-a-service
  • the last market Compute & Energy Infrastructure
    Even if AGI arrives, all remaining compute work reduces to inference; it is the terminal market that subsumes everything else
  • custom models
    Not fine-tuned from scratch but open-source base models modified via post-training, quantization, or compilation for a specific use case — 95%+ of Baseten's tokens
  • post-training
    RL-based and other techniques applied after base model training to specialize a model on a company's proprietary reward signal/user data
  • user signal Moats & Defensibility
    Proprietary data generated by end-users interacting with a product (e.g., clinician edits in Abridge) that only that company can access — the moat for the application layer
  • runtime fabric Compute & Energy Infrastructure
    Baseten's abstraction layer across 18 clouds / 90 clusters that enables failover, latency optimization, and rapid onboarding of new compute providers

Arguments

The application layer will survive against the frontier labs strength: high
Explicit premises:
  • Companies have unique user signal that labs cannot access
  • Proprietary user signal enables post-training on unique reward functions
  • Workflows (not just model calls) encode company-specific value
Implicit premises:
  • Labs will not acquire or replicate the distribution needed to gather domain-specific user signal at scale
  • Regulatory and data-access barriers will persist, especially in healthcare
Inferences:
  • The value accrues to companies closest to the user, not closest to the weights
Inference is 'the last market' — it cannot be competed away strength: high
Explicit premises:
  • Even with AGI, everything reduces to inference
  • Jevons paradox means cheaper inference drives more demand, not less
  • Customers never reach 'enough intelligence' — they always want better answers
Implicit premises:
  • Training will eventually become a solved/commoditized upstream step while inference remains the ongoing operational cost
  • The intelligence demand curve has no natural ceiling
Inferences:
  • Inference companies may have more durable moats than training/lab companies
Owning compute is the dominant competitive strategy in inference strength: high
Explicit premises:
  • Supply crunch is severe and worsening
  • Quality suppliers are a small subset — maybe 3-4 'gold tier' clouds
  • Long-term contracts now require 3-5 year terms with 20% TCV prepaid
  • Low cost of capital enables compute acquisition which enables demand capture
Implicit premises:
  • The supply crunch will persist long enough for compute-owning companies to build durable advantages
  • Demand growth will outpace supply growth for the foreseeable future
Inferences:
  • This creates a capital-intensive dynamic more like energy/telecom than traditional SaaS, favoring public companies or well-capitalized private ones
The US must develop strong domestic open-source models strength: medium
Explicit premises:
  • Five Chinese labs are producing open-source models; the US struggles to match
  • If the US loses access to cheap open-source intelligence, innovation slows
  • DeepSeek runs at ~20% the cost of comparable models with similar quality
Implicit premises:
  • Geopolitical tensions could restrict access to Chinese models in the future
  • Open-source models are critical infrastructure, not just nice-to-have
Inferences:
  • The US open-source AI gap is a national security concern, not just a market concern

Narratives

  • Abridge (ambient medical scribe) as an example of unique user signal — clinicians' edits to notes and downstream EMR workflows create data only Abridge can access, making it defensible against labs
    → Supports the proposition that the application layer survives because of proprietary user signal
  • Baseten runs 90 clusters across 18 clouds and can onboard a new provider in half a day; they hold a daily 4pm capacity meeting
    → Supports the proposition that operational excellence and multi-cloud flexibility are key competitive advantages in the supply crunch
  • Meeting with senior AWS executives where all their pagers went off multiple times in 45 minutes
    → Normalizes the ops-heavy, always-on culture as inherent to infrastructure companies — not a dysfunction but a selection mechanism
  • Co-founder's 7-year-old asking 'Is that a P0?' when the pager goes off
    → Reinforces the ops-culture narrative with humor — infrastructure is a lifestyle, not just a job
  • Tuhin once suggested to Sarah Guo that Baseten should hire only engineers instead of salespeople
    → Illustrates his evolution as a founder — from engineering purism to recognizing the necessity of leadership and go-to-market roles
  • The PaLM acquisition — a customer doing post-training on Baseten realized they'd need to become an inference company; Baseten realized they needed research capability
    → Supports the argument that post-training and inference are converging into one problem
  • Someone told Tuhin after the DeepSeek moment: 'We should just forget this is a Chinese model and act like it came from Meta'
    → Supports the argument that model origin matters less than model capability and availability

Questions addressed

  • ✅ Will the independent application layer survive against the frontier labs?
    Yes — unique user signal and downstream workflows create defensible positions that labs cannot replicate
  • ✅ Should companies use Chinese open-source models despite geopolitical concerns?
    Yes — no evidence of embedded threats; the real risk is NOT having access to cheap open-source intelligence
  • ✅ When should companies invest in custom models?
    After proving product-market fit with frontier models — 'no post-training pre-PMF'
  • ✅ What creates a dominant player in inference?
    Software layer stickiness + compute ownership + operational excellence — not any single factor
  • ⭕ Will alternative chips challenge Nvidia?
    Inevitable long-term, but near-term (2-3 years) Nvidia's ecosystem and supply chain are unassailable
  • ✅ Does cheaper inference cannibalize inference revenue?
    No — Jevons paradox; developers insert more intelligence, agents run longer, demand expands
  • ⭕ What is the long-term equilibrium of AI adoption?
    'Concierge everything' — but specifics of economic and social impact left unexplored
