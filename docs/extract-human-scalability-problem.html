Presentation: The Human Scalability Problem: Why Your Teams Don't Scale Like Your Code

Charlotte de Jong Schouwenburg · presentation (conference talk transcript) · 2026-05-09 source ↗ Reader 🖥️

Coordination CostOrchestration Tax / Human Review BottleneckPsychological SafetyTeam AutonomyTrust
When organizations scale, technical systems scale but human cooperation breaks down due to communication overload, loss of shared context, and the non-replicability of trust — and the fix requires deliberate investment in communication architecture, engineered trust, distributed cohesion, and leadership that addresses relational dynamics rather than just content.

practical · presentation (conference talk with Q&A) — A practitioner talk by a business psychologist specializing in tech companies. Blends applied psychology theory (Dunbar's number, psychological safety, trust) with concrete interventions. Includes audience interaction and real company case studies (LeanIX, Spotify, Google). Primarily prescriptive — diagnoses the human scaling problem then offers a toolkit.

Key terms

  • behavioral scalability Coordination Cost
    The ability of human cooperation patterns — communication, trust, coordination — to remain effective as team size and organizational complexity grow
  • human latency Orchestration Tax / Human Review Bottleneck
    The time it takes to move from problem identification to solution to action; analogous to system latency but for human decision-making and coordination
  • relationship bank account Trust
    A metaphor for accumulated relational capital in any interpersonal connection; positive deposits (personal connection, understanding) allow withdrawals (feedback, criticism) without destroying the relationship
  • trust (in this context) Trust
    The belief that others will act with integrity and reliability even when you're struggling; enables open feedback and honest communication without fear of exclusion
  • psychological safety Psychological Safety
    The shared belief that one can take interpersonal risks within the group — make mistakes, propose ideas, admit ignorance — without punishment; described as the 'funnel of trust' requiring trust as foundation
  • cultural drift
    The divergence of shared identity, norms, and definitions (e.g., 'done', 'moving fast') across locations and subcultures as an organization scales, especially internationally
  • communication architecture Coordination Cost
    The deliberate design of how information flows across teams — which channels, what frequency, what content — as opposed to ad hoc or emergent communication patterns

Propositions

  • explicitTechnical systems scale readily, but human cooperation does not scale automatically — it actively degrades without deliberate intervention.conf: high
  • explicitHumans can sustain approximately 150 social relationships (Dunbar's number), which imposes a hard cognitive limit on organizational scaling through informal coordination.conf: medium
  • explicitTrust cannot be replicated by copying organizational structure; even if you duplicate an autonomous team's structure perfectly, the new team must build trust from scratch.conf: high
  • explicitPsychological safety requires trust as a foundation but goes further — it is the belief that interpersonal risk-taking is safe within the group.conf: high
  • explicitdevelopedTeams given autonomy cannot harness it productively until trust and psychological safety are established.conf: high
    extends ←Your AI strategy has a trust problem, not a tooling problem · p8 — Adds a precondition for usable autonomy beyond trust and psychological safety: employees may have been trained out of exercising agency and cannot simply absorb it when granted.
  • explicitMost organizational firefighting addresses symptoms (missed deadlines, non-collaboration) rather than root causes (trust deficit, lost shared context, communication overload).conf: high
  • explicitRepetition of core messages across multiple channels and times is essential because information overload means single-transmission communication reliably fails at scale.conf: high
  • explicitNew teams must invest in relationship-building before task work; going directly into project deadlines creates negative relational bank accounts that produce conflict and poor collaboration.conf: high
  • explicitLeaders must model the vulnerability and transparency they want from their teams — walking the talk is non-negotiable for building trust.conf: high
  • explicitRituals and predictability calm the human brain and create the conditions for trust; they function as social infrastructure that persists even when team composition changes.conf: medium
  • implicitAddressing fear and emotional concerns directly (e.g., layoff anxiety) by providing space for conversation — not by solving the content problem — restores enough regulation for people to function.conf: medium
  • explicitThe leader's primary job at scale shifts from content/task management to facilitating the human system — ensuring teams can do their best work rather than directing the work itself.conf: high

Arguments

Organizations must deliberately invest in human scalability, not just technical scalability
You can't replicate trust by replicating structure
Relationship investment must precede task work for new teams
Leaders should address relational dynamics rather than just content/symptoms

Narratives


  • → Provides the primary case study and makes the abstract problem concrete and research-backed

  • → Demonstrates that even best-practice structural solutions fail without relational investment — the 'you can copy-paste autonomy but not trust' lesson

  • → Shows that trust is fragile and tied to specific social configurations, not transferable

  • → Illustrates cultural drift as a concrete, vivid example of lost shared context

  • → Real-world validation from the audience; shows the trust-then-safety progression in practice

Gaps

  • practicalNo quantitative framework for when to invest in human scalability interventions. The talk says companies often come to Bravely at 100-150 people when employee surveys flag problems, but offers no guidance on investment timing, budget, or ROI metrics.
  • theoreticalThe relationship between AI/automation and human scalability is acknowledged ('AI is doing a lot') but immediately dismissed ('this will still always be true'). No exploration of how AI tools might change the communication overload or shared context problems — or whether they introduce new trust challenges.
    closed byYour AI strategy has a trust problem, not a tooling problem — Explores what the talk dismissed: AI agents distribute context and shrink the middle-management translation problem, shifting the binding constraint of scaling from communication overload to trust and permission structures.
  • evidentialThe talk relies heavily on the Spotify squad model and Google Project Aristotle as examples, but these are well-known cases that may not generalize. No counter-examples are discussed — organizations that scaled human cooperation successfully without these specific interventions.
  • theoreticalDunbar's number is presented as a hard cognitive limit, but the speaker herself notes it was 'measured on neurotypical people' and is 'a guess.' The implications of neurodiversity for organizational scaling are raised but not explored.
  • practicalThe interventions (communication architecture, trust engineering, distributed cohesion) are presented as a menu of techniques without a theory of sequencing or prioritization. Which matters most at 50 people vs. 200 vs. 1000? The talk doesn't say.
