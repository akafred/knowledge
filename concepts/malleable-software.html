Malleable Software

Software that users can reshape from within — including cheap, disposable, self-made tools — revived as AI collapses the cost of modification.

Also known as: malleable software, Internal Reprogrammability, reimagining, disposable software, Sketches, sketches

  • Bliki: Vibe Coding Martin Fowler · 2026-05-21 source 🖥️
    Vibe coding — building software by prompting an LLM without ever looking at the generated code — is a distinct practice from agentic programming, useful only for disposable software, because forgetting the code exists produces serious risks in security, maintainability, and correctness.
  • Why cultivating agency matters more than cultivating skills in the AI era | Max Schoening (Head of Product, Notion) Lenny Rachitsky, Max Schoening · 2026-05-09 source 🖥️
    In the AI era, the differentiating factor for product builders is not skill acquisition but agency — the willingness to treat the world as malleable and to make things — and the products that endure are those built around a tiny, exceptionally good core rather than an accumulation of features.
  • Applying Best Simple System for Now for Software Design Ben Linders · 2026-05-09 source 🖥️
    The trade-off between technical debt and missed deadlines is a false dichotomy; by building the simplest system that meets only current requirements to a high standard (Best Simple System for Now), teams can ship continuously while keeping future change cheap — because simplicity, not speculative flexibility, is the real source of adaptability.
  • Fragments: May 5 Martin Fowler · 2026-05-05 source 🖥️
    The current moment in AI-assisted software development is defined by a tension between the power of cloud-hosted AI genies and the risks they carry — to code quality, to truth, to budgets, and to autonomy — and the pieces Fowler curates collectively ask whether the escape route runs through disciplined engineering practice, local-first computing, or some combination of both.
  • Radar Trends to Watch: May 2026 Mike Loukides and Claude · 2026-04-20 source 🖥️
    The AI industry is rapidly moving from experimental models to operational infrastructure — agents, orchestration layers, and security tooling — and the central tension is between open deployment and controlled access, a tension most visible in the divergent approaches to frontier security capabilities taken by Anthropic (restrict) and OpenAI (release).

Structural barriers to agency (economic precarity, organizational culture, psychological safety, access to tools) are absent from the analysis. The advice to 'just make things' assumes access and psychological conditions that are themselves unevenly distributed.
