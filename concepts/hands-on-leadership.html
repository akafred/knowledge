Hands-On Leadership & the Pendulum

Deliberately moving between leading and delivering across a career — technical depth as the entry ticket that keeps leadership grounded, with real identity grief in the transition.

Also known as: pendle (verb) / pendel (noun), pendle, Engineer-Manager Pendulum, inngangsbillett (entry ticket), inngangsbillett, lederhatten / brette opp armene, sorgprosess (developer-to-leader), joggeskoene på (put your running shoes on)

  • 4) Morten Eikeland: Det finnes alltid et hierarki Truls Jørgensen og Audun Fauchald Strand — Heis Podcast · 2026-06-01 source ↗ Reader 🖥️
    Hierarchy is not optional in any group of more than a few people — it arises structurally from division-of-labour pressure plus external groups' need for a single point of contact — and the agile/teknologi/flat-struktur ideology that pretends otherwise does not eliminate hierarchy, it only makes the actual operating hierarchy invisible, unspoken, and harder to challenge; therefore the work of leading well is not to flatten but to operate at the correct abstraction level for the role one is in, to recognise the role-vs-person distinction, and (for a developer becoming a leader) to walk a real sorgprosess of identity loss before gaining a different kind of relevance.
  • Itchy Brain (Still Burning: Michael Grinich & Kent Beck on AI adoption, enterprise developer tools) Kent Beck — Still Burning Podcast · 2026-05-20 Reader 🖥️
    We are not in another technology hype wave but in a step-function transformation: the marginal cost of producing software is collapsing toward zero, large incumbents are adopting AI faster than any prior shift, and the whole ecosystem — not just the AI labs — is accelerating; in this 'Red Queen' regime, profit and productivity may not visibly improve because every competitor is running at the new tempo, so the only sane response is to paddle straight into the wave by sustaining the maker's compulsion (the 'itchy brain'), reshaping engineering leadership toward hands-on impact, and accepting that software engineering as a profession will resemble medicine or law more than the unique 2010s template that minted six-figure undergrads.
