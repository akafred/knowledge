Local AI

Running AI models on hardware you control — laptops, phones, own servers — for cost, privacy, latency and autonomy reasons.

Also known as: local AI, Local AI, local models, on-device, small LLM, small LLMs, Edge models (E2B, E4B), local execution (Claude Cowork), Model as a Service (Scaleway)

Documents (10)

  • Experiences with local models for coding Birgitta Böckeler · 2026-07-08 source ↗ Reader 🖥️
    Running small, locally-hosted open models for agentic coding is not yet a plug-and-play substitute for frontier models — it remains frustrating and far below frontier capability, and is viable today only for a narrow class of small, well-scoped, often pre-planned tasks, with the choice of task (more than the model or harness) being the dominant factor that determines whether it works.
  • Cybersecurity and the Gap Between Skill and Ability Bruce Schneier · 2026-06-29 source ↗ Reader 🖥️
    Computers, and now AI far faster, are decoupling ability (the capacity to accomplish something) from skill (mastery earned through practice) — and because the norm-instilling process of acquiring skill no longer gates dangerous capability, the only workable response to democratized offensive AI is to harness AI for the defense.
  • Gemma 4 QAT models: Optimizing model compression for mobile and laptop efficiency Omar Sanseviero (Google) · 2026-06-06 Reader 🖥️
    Gemma 4's new QAT checkpoints — especially a custom mobile-quantization schema — make small, high-quality on-device LLMs practical on phones and consumer GPUs, so developers should run Gemma locally rather than in the cloud.
  • 95% cheaper and sovereign AI: Why we dropped Copilot at Coop Bank — LinkedIn post by Jannik William Frederiksen Jannik William Frederiksen · 2026-06-06 Reader 🖥️
    Coop Bank rejected Microsoft Copilot because EU data sovereignty (bank-regulated data possibly egressing the EU under peak load) and cost (200,000 DKK/year for 80 users) failed the bar for a bank; instead they wired Scaleway-hosted open-weight models (Gemma/Mistral) behind a one-day custom chatbot UI, achieving full EU data residency and a >95% annual cost reduction (under 10,000 DKK) — at the price of not being a 1-1 Copilot replacement, but with hyper-flexibility that lets business ideas reach production in moments; the implicit argument is that for regulated EU institutions the default Microsoft path is both more expensive and less sovereign than a hosted-OSS path, and that vendor-default thinking is leaving competitive lead on the table.
  • GitHub - Doorman11991/smallcode: AI coding agent optimized for small LLMs. 87% benchmark with 4B-active model. https://github.com/Doorman11991/ · 2026-05-21 source ↗ Reader 🖥️
    A well-engineered harness — context budgeting, forgiving tool-call parsing, patch-first editing, two-stage tool routing, decomposed planning, and per-model profiles — can extract production-grade agentic coding work from small (7B-20B), locally-hosted LLMs that would otherwise be considered too weak for the task, making local, private, frontier-free coding agents viable.
  • Local AI Mike Loukides and Claude · 2026-05-09 source ↗ Reader 🖥️

  • Fragments: May 5 Martin Fowler · 2026-05-05 source ↗ Reader 🖥️
    The current moment in AI-assisted software development is defined by a tension between the power of cloud-hosted AI genies and the risks they carry — to code quality, to truth, to budgets, and to autonomy — and the pieces Fowler curates collectively ask whether the escape route runs through disciplined engineering practice, local-first computing, or some combination of both.
  • Hugging Face's Clem Delangue: Stop Comparing Engines to Cars Turing Post (Ksenia Se interviewing Clem Delangue) · 2026-05-02 source ↗ Reader 🖥️

  • Building AI Agents For The Enterprise Anthropic (vendor-published; Reader recorded the byline as 'Claude') · 2025 source ↗ Reader 🖥️
