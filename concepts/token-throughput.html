Token Throughput

The rate at which a person or organization can productively consume AI inference tokens — the new utilization metric, and a metric that invites gaming.

Also known as: token throughput, Token throughput, token burn, tokenmaxxing, tokens (as consumed by CMOs)

    Cursor's two years of aggregated usage data show that AI-coding output is extremely power-law skewed across developers, that its cost is dominated by input/context-reading tokens (making caching decisive), and that developers are rapidly abandoning manual review of AI-written commits.
  • Agentics / Tech Things: Tokenmaxxing is dead, long live tokenmaxxing theahura · 2026-06-27 Reader 🖥️
    Tokenmaxxing — deliberately spending large amounts of AI inference tokens — was born as a purposeful blunt-force tactic to force AI adoption past resistant employees and died once vendors withdrew subsidies and raised prices, but it will be reborn on genuine-ROI footing because a new 'compounding correctness' regime (more tokens now reliably yield better results, making unsupervised 24/7 agent loops worth running) plus cheap open models run in loops make maximal token spend economically rational rather than mere incentive-gaming.
  • This Week in AI: Production Viability Michelle Smith (O'Reilly Network) · 2026-06-06 Reader 🖥️
    AI's current bottleneck is not capability but measurement: practitioners and organizations are producing prodigious output (tokens, code, recommendations, profiles) while lacking the contextual judgment, metacognitive discipline, and incentive structures needed to tell which output is actually worth shipping.
