Goodhart's Law

The principle that once a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure — the mechanism by which AI-era activity metrics (deploy frequency, PR volume, velocity) get gamed or inflated without the underlying quality they were meant to proxy.

Also known as: Goodhart's Law, when a measure becomes a target, metric gaming

  • The 8 software engineering metrics AI broke Michael Hill · 2026-06-16 source ↗ Reader 🖥️
    Software engineering metrics were built on the assumption that human effort and output move in rough proportion, and AI has severed that link by making activity nearly free — so activity/volume proxies (deployment frequency, cycle time, PR and commit volume, lines of code, satisfaction, efficiency/flow, DevEx/DXI) now measure tool adoption rather than engineering quality, while only outcome-based measures (time to recover, escaped defect rate, delivered value) remain trustworthy, because they measure what happened rather than how much happened.
