Professional Identity

The self-concept practitioners build around their craft — threatened when AI obsoletes defining skills, and requiring deliberate reconstruction rather than denial.

Also known as: identity threat, professional identity, Privilegium, ikke identitet, kompetansebygging

Documents (6)

  • Lenny Rachitsky's Post Lenny Rachitsky · 2026-07-10 source ↗ Reader 🖥️
    The question that best predicts how a tech worker feels about their work in 2026 is no longer their role or employer but what AI has done to their sense of professional identity — and workers sort into four distinct emotional archetypes (Energized, Conflicted, Disoriented, Resentful) that form a spectrum from optimism to resistance.
  • Why product feels so hard right now - Charity Ibhadon (WPP) Mind the Product · 2026-07-08 source ↗ Reader 🖥️
    Product work feels harder than ever not mainly because the job changed but because a culture with no 'gold standard' fuses people's identity to the work and teaches them to mistake the physical symptoms of burnout for proof of excellence; the way through is to de-identify from work, defend recovery and boundaries, and lead through durable, unflappable 'happy high status' composure and commercial curiosity rather than chasing ever more frameworks and certificates.
  • KI, utvikling og følelser Vidar Moe · 2026-05-21 source ↗ Reader 🖥️
    Generative AI is reshaping software development less as a productivity story and more as an emotional one — moving developers from building to specifying systems, and the central professional question is no longer how to ship faster but how to keep developers from becoming alienated, dulled, and burnt out, which requires deliberately keeping humans inside the loop through code-the-foundation workshops, explicit TDD with the agent, and a psychological reframe from programming to problem-solving.
  • Motivated reasoning Mark Seemann · 2026-05-04 source ↗ Reader 🖥️
    The author's recent criticisms of AI in software development are likely shaped by motivated reasoning — love of craft and economic self-interest — yet even if ninety percent of his concerns prove irrelevant, the remaining ones may still matter, and raising counterpoints to techno-optimism has value regardless.
  • TBM 420: The AI Playbook Puzzle John Cutler from The Beautiful Mess · 2026-04-20 Reader 🖥️
    Nobody has an AI playbook yet because the real challenge is not tactical AI adoption but the identity-threatening work of distinguishing which existing practices were always broken, which were genuinely good, which only worked due to now-vanished constraints, and developing the meta-skill of contextual judgment to navigate all three.
  • 7) Stine Haugseth: Insourcing, retning og strategi på en ølbrikke Truls Jørgensen og Audun Fauchald Strand — Heis Podcast · 2025-12-15 source ↗ Reader 🖥️
    Insourcing, retning, and strategi-på-en-ølbrikke are three faces of one leadership move: pulling the locus of decision-making in-house and then compressing direction tightly enough that a distributed organisation can actually act on it — insourcing repatriates the people who must own the work, oppdragsbasert ledelse and sirkler av autonomi give those people a clear mission with explicit frihetsgrenser, and the ølbrikke forces utviklingsstrategi down to the few hovedområder a development organisation can actually carry, with OKRs underneath and an explicit business case underneath that.

But this conflates aesthetic preference with professional identity. For some developers, the satisfaction is in correctness, reliability, or deep debugging, not greenfield design. The analysis would be richer with a more nuanced model of what developers value.
