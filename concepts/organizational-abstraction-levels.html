Organizational Abstraction Levels

Treating organizations as stacked abstraction layers and leading at the level appropriate to one's role — a software metaphor applied to management.

Also known as: abstraksjonslag, lede på riktig nivå, å lede på riktig nivå, abstraction layers, altitude (of review), presisjon i abstraksjonen / å treffe på riktig abstraksjonsnivå, retenking av abstraksjonsnivå (rethinking the abstraction level)

Truls Jørgensen og Audun Fauchald Strand — Heis Podcast · 2026-06-01		abstraksjonslagBorrowed from software engineering by analogy. Each level of an organisation (individ → team → område → divisjon → selskap) is a distinct abstraction layer with its own time horizon, granularity, and language. To lead at level N means to reason at level N's granularity and time horizon, not at level N-1's. A leder must abstrahere upward and forward in time (morgen, not akkurat nå), explicitly giving up konkretisering. The skill of moving between abstraction layers is the developer's transferable instinct for leadership; Printzell Halvorsen (ep 8) explicitly cites Eikeland on this point as the operational meaning of 'å lede på riktig nivå.'

Truls Jørgensen og Audun Fauchald Strand — Heis Podcast · 2026-06-01		lede på riktig nivåOperating at the abstraction layer appropriate to the role one occupies. For a leder of 8+, this means giving up detail focus and detailed konkretisering, generalising the gruppe, and operating with a longer time horizon. The discipline is to remain interested in lower levels without intervening at them. Failing to lede på riktig nivå produces two characteristic errors: micromanaging from above (drar detalj-fokus opp), or overdreven selvsetthet at the abstract level (illusion of explanatory depth).
