Change Management & Adoption

The discipline of institutionalizing organizational change so it becomes durable — urgency, coalition, vision, communication, quick wins, and structural anchoring (Kotter, Fearless Change, ADKAR, Lewin) — the org-wide arc distinct from the tactical upward influence of managing-up.

Also known as: change management, change adoption, Kotter's 8 steps, Kotter, Fearless Change, ADKAR, Lewin, make it stick, organizational change adoption, institutionalizing change

  • Your AI agent just blamed the network team. Now what? Nik Kale · 2026-06-15 source ↗ Reader 🖥️
    Deploying autonomous, cross-domain AI diagnostic agents in production incident response introduces a new organizational-political risk -- a confidently-asserted, team-implicating finding that may be contested -- and that risk is best managed not by better AI reasoning but by a deliberately staged trust-building process (investigation-scoped credentials, a read-only start, an auditable reasoning trail, and treating rollout as organizational change with pre-secured cross-team buy-in) that earns autonomy incrementally rather than being engineered away as a purely technical problem.
