Systemic Apprenticeship & Skill Transmission

Deliberately engineering apprenticeship into everyday work — senior-as-teacher role expectations, deliberate exposure to higher-stakes decisions, systematic (not personality-dependent) mentorship — as the mechanism by which junior/emerging talent acquires craft, distinct from episodic training programs.

Also known as: apprenticeship, apprenticeship pipeline, junior development, skill transmission, craft transmission, teaching in the flow of work, development happens in the work
