Industrialization of AI

AI's one-way evolutionary shift to commodity utility — a step-function regime change in which everyone runs faster just to stay in place.

Also known as: industrialisation (of ML), industrialisation of ML, industrialization phase (of AI), growing up, componentisation, Red Queen (evolutionary theory), Red Queen, step function shift (vs. acceleration), engineering vs craft (in software)

  • Itchy Brain (Still Burning: Michael Grinich & Kent Beck on AI adoption, enterprise developer tools) Kent Beck — Still Burning Podcast · 2026-05-20 Reader 🖥️
    We are not in another technology hype wave but in a step-function transformation: the marginal cost of producing software is collapsing toward zero, large incumbents are adopting AI faster than any prior shift, and the whole ecosystem — not just the AI labs — is accelerating; in this 'Red Queen' regime, profit and productivity may not visibly improve because every competitor is running at the new tempo, so the only sane response is to paddle straight into the wave by sustaining the maker's compulsion (the 'itchy brain'), reshaping engineering leadership toward hands-on impact, and accepting that software engineering as a profession will resemble medicine or law more than the unique 2010s template that minted six-figure undergrads.
  • The Week AI Grew Up AI Daily Brief · 2026-05-01 source ↗ Reader 🖥️
    The AI industry crossed from subsidized experimentation into mature economic infrastructure in the week of April 28, 2026 — visible simultaneously in pricing model shifts, cloud earnings, geopolitical treatment, and the industrialization of agent harnesses — and everyone from developers to governments must now operate accordingly.
  • Simon Wardley's Post — Industrialisation of ML: nine notes on what to expect Simon Wardley · 2025-12 Reader 🖥️
    What is happening with AI is the industrialisation of machine learning — a one-way evolutionary shift to commodity/utility — and the right posture toward it is neither resistance nor hype but disciplined adaptation through the predictable 5-8 year co-evolution of practices, with explicit attention to where bottlenecks, supply-chain power, and inequality will actually land.
