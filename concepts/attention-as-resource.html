Attention as a Hijackable Resource

Attention treated as a finite, depletable resource that engineered systems (feeds, notifications, ads, autoplay) capture by design — and the friction and infrastructure required to protect and reclaim it as a precondition for thinking.

Also known as: attention economy, reclaiming attention, attention as a resource, attention capture, engineered distraction, persuasive design, digital self-control, attention infrastructure, deep work, Stolen Focus
