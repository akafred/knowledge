Craft Community

Self-organizing professional communities and protected craft time through which engineering practice is transmitted and renewed.

Also known as: fagmiljø / community, fagmiljø, fagtid, communities of practice, tech-møtet, the playbook

Documents (4)

  • You Don't Get to Create Anything (Still Burning: Beck + Randy Shoup on Jevons paradox and distributed systems under AI) Kent Beck — Still Burning Podcast · 2026-06-03 source 🖥️
    Cognition is becoming near-free, and per Jevons paradox that will produce more cognitive work and more engineers, not fewer — but the people best prepared for this regime are exactly the ones who already lived through writing the distributed-systems playbook, because they know two things the panicking crowd forgets: that a playbook is hard-won and not obvious until written, and that the human compulsion underneath all of this is the maker's need to create the thing rather than merely transcribe it (the 'you don't get to create anything' moment that drives Shoup out of patent law and back into engineering); accordingly the right posture is calm, perspective-keeping, deductive engagement with the new regime — bounding the genie with specs/evals, making good practices easier rather than shaming the bad, and treating this as another wave of physics-discovery rather than a hype cycle or an apocalypse.
  • Developing Your Leadership Skills toward Principal Engineering Ben Linders · 2026-04-15 source 🖥️
    Becoming a principal engineer requires deliberately cultivating leadership skills beyond technical expertise — especially by bringing skills from outside work, engaging in tech communities, and public speaking — and these activities create compounding positive feedback loops that accelerate career growth.
  • 13) Tobias K. Torrissen om kodekvalitet, konferanser og AI Truls Jørgensen og Audun Fauchald Strand — Heis Podcast · 2026-03-16 source 🖥️
    In the age of LLM-generated code, the durable value of an engineering leader is not in writing code but in cultivating the engineering practices, explicit design discipline, and craft community that determine whether a long-lived system can be understood, maintained, and adapted — and code quality, understood as 'obvious over clever,' matters more in this era, not less.
  • 5) Vidar Moe: Det eneste Vidar vil er å ha det lett Truls Jørgensen og Audun Fauchald Strand — Heis Podcast · 2025-11-17 source 🖥️
    Vidar Moe's core engineering-leadership philosophy is 'gjøre det lett og rett' (make it easy and right) — a Toyota-Way-inflected craft and leadership stance in which you lead an organization most powerfully not by exhortation but by patiently building the tooling, frameworks, fagtid, hiring filters, fixed-term leadership rotation, and shared decision arenas that make the right thing the path of least resistance; the episode's ironic title ('the only thing Vidar wants is to have it easy') inverts this — Moe wants the work to be easy precisely because he is willing to do the very hard, decade-long, mostly invisible work of removing friction from doing the right thing, and his current concern with AI agents is the natural next chapter of the same project.
