Inference Optimization

Techniques — quantization, caching, speculative decoding, sparse architectures — that cut the compute cost and latency of serving models.

Also known as: speculative decoding, quantization, Quantization-Aware Training (QAT), QAT, Post-Training Quantization (PTQ), KV cache reuse, Mixture of Experts (MoE), mixture-of-experts (MoE), MoE, time to first token, Multi-Token Prediction (MTP)

  • The Pulse: Interesting AI coding stats from Cursor The Pragmatic Engineer (Gergely Orosz) · 2026-07-09 Reader 🖥️
    Cursor's two years of aggregated usage data show that AI-coding output is extremely power-law skewed across developers, that its cost is dominated by input/context-reading tokens (making caching decisive), and that developers are rapidly abandoning manual review of AI-written commits.
  • Experiences with local models for coding Birgitta Böckeler · 2026-07-08 source ↗ Reader 🖥️
    Running small, locally-hosted open models for agentic coding is not yet a plug-and-play substitute for frontier models — it remains frustrating and far below frontier capability, and is viable today only for a narrow class of small, well-scoped, often pre-planned tasks, with the choice of task (more than the model or harness) being the dominant factor that determines whether it works.
  • Gemma 4 QAT models: Optimizing model compression for mobile and laptop efficiency Omar Sanseviero (Google) · 2026-06-06 Reader 🖥️
    Gemma 4's new QAT checkpoints — especially a custom mobile-quantization schema — make small, high-quality on-device LLMs practical on phones and consumer GPUs, so developers should run Gemma locally rather than in the cloud.
  • Local AI Mike Loukides and Claude · 2026-05-09 source ↗ Reader 🖥️
    Open-weight AI models running on local hardware have become viable alternatives to cloud-based frontier models for a growing majority of use cases, driven by converging forces of cost, privacy, regulatory compliance, fine-tuning flexibility, and the needs of a global developer community that US cloud providers have largely ignored.
  • Guest post: AI Inference Is Breaking Unit Economics Turing Post · 2026-05-07 source ↗ Reader 🖥️
    AI products scale like software but cost like infrastructure, and the gap between those curves — unit economics dominated by per-inference cost — is now the binding constraint on whether AI businesses can scale sustainably; closing that gap requires stacking optimization techniques (caching, quantization, speculative decoding, smart routing) that are increasingly accessible to non-hyperscaler teams.
  • DeepSeek v4, and the end of the OpenAI/Microsoft AGI clause Simon Willison · 2026-04-30 Reader 🖥️
    A snapshot of late-April 2026 in AI: a Chinese open-weights lab is now pricing the frontier into the floor, the AGI clause that supposedly governed the OpenAI/Microsoft relationship has been quietly written out of existence, and Willison's own LLM tooling has to be rebuilt around the assumption that model output is no longer just text — together describing an industry where the economics, the legal scaffolding, and the developer abstractions are all reorganizing around the same new reality of multi-modal, agentic, commoditized models.
  • OpenAI Introduces Websocket-Based Execution Mode to Reduce Latency in Agentic Workflows Leela Kumili · 2026-04-20 source ↗ Reader 🖥️
    Transport-layer optimization — replacing per-step HTTP round-trips with a persistent WebSocket connection — yields substantial latency reductions (30-40%) in agentic AI workflows, demonstrating that system-level infrastructure changes outside the model itself are now a primary lever for improving real-world AI performance.
