    Norway's understanding of AI has been hijacked by the chatbot — both as the dominant interface and as the dominant mental model — and this collapses our maturation path: we substitute buying foreign LLM products for the slower, harder work of in-house machine-learning, problem framing, and explainability, even though the technical theory (data manifolds, approximation, neurosymbolic combinations) and the geopolitics both demand that we develop our own capability rather than outsource our problem-solving.
  • The Psychological Costs of Adopting AI Guy Champniss · 2026-05 source 🖥️
    AI adoption strategies that focus only on productivity ignore six forms of 'psychological debt' (cognitive, autonomy, competency, relatedness, credibility, identity) that AI use imposes on employees, and unless companies deliberately design the human-AI relationship to mitigate these debts — through cognitive friction, explainability, peer-led training, visible normalization of AI use, and identity-affirming framings — the psychological cost will cancel out the productivity gains, making adoption a human-infrastructure problem as much as a technology problem.
