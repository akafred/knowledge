Documents (4)

  • The New Rules for Becoming an Enterprise Leader HBR.org · 2026-07-14 source ↗ Reader 🖥️
    Three converging forces — pervasive personal/organizational use of AI, geopolitical turbulence, and compressed leadership pipelines — have made every one of the seven classic functional-to-enterprise leadership transitions harder and faster than before, so organizations must replace 'ready now' succession planning with a 'ready enough plus support' model that deliberately builds both outer leader skills (strategic thinking, decision architecture, stakeholder navigation) and inner leader skills (polarity tolerance, emotional regulation, real-time self-awareness) earlier in careers, through curated crucible experiences rather than credentialing.
  • Our Favorite Management Tips on Giving Feedback HBR Editors · 2026-05-21 Reader 🖥️
    Feedback works when it separates the person from the behavior and is delivered inside a relationship the recipient already trusts: define what feedback must never do (attack character), structure it around a specific behavior plus its impact plus a next step, invite it proactively rather than only issuing it top-down, calibrate its intensity and timing to the relationship's maturity and the issue's severity, and treat it as a two-way, purpose-conferring conversation rather than a one-way verdict. The eight tips are different tactical answers to one underlying claim: feedback's effectiveness is gated by psychological safety and behavioral specificity, not by the giver's honesty or courage alone.
  • Communicating with Confidence When You're Under Pressure HBR.org · 2026-05-07 source ↗ Reader 🖥️
    Effective communication under pressure requires shifting focus from your internal state (fatigue, stress, emotional resistance) to the outcome you want to create and the impact on your audience — and this shift is enabled by self-awareness practices (checking in before you speak), structural discipline (anchoring to purpose, being concise), and treating self-care as something that happens outside the communication moment so you can prioritize the other person during it.
  • When You Start to Find Employee Requests Irritating Ron Carucci and Jay Stringer · 2026-04-10 source ↗ Reader 🖥️
    A leader's disproportionate irritation at employee requests is diagnostic data about their own unmet core needs — safety, love, belonging, and meaning — and effective leadership requires stabilizing those needs in oneself before responding to others.
