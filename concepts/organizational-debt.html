Organizational Debt

Accumulated structural and coordination liabilities in how an organization is arranged — the sibling of technical debt.

Also known as: organizational debt, organisatorisk gjeld

    Vidar Moe's core engineering-leadership philosophy is 'gjøre det lett og rett' (make it easy and right) — a Toyota-Way-inflected craft and leadership stance in which you lead an organization most powerfully not by exhortation but by patiently building the tooling, frameworks, fagtid, hiring filters, fixed-term leadership rotation, and shared decision arenas that make the right thing the path of least resistance; the episode's ironic title ('the only thing Vidar wants is to have it easy') inverts this — Moe wants the work to be easy precisely because he is willing to do the very hard, decade-long, mostly invisible work of removing friction from doing the right thing, and his current concern with AI agents is the natural next chapter of the same project.

  • incompleteStorey's Triple Debt Model is imported uncritically as the organizing taxonomy. But it is not obviously exhaustive: process debt (the way work flows through the team), dependency debt (third-party choices whose rationale and constraints aren't recorded), data debt (schema and semantic-meaning decisions), and organizational debt (decisions about team structure and ownership) are all candidate fourth (fifth, sixth) categories. The independence claim that does so much work in the post depends on the taxonomy being clean — if intent debt overlaps significantly with one of these others, the agent-paydown asymmetry argument may not generalize cleanly.
