Cognitive Load

The mental burden work imposes on humans — a design target for platforms and AI systems alike, since extraneous load cancels out AI's quality contributions.

Also known as: cognitive load, Cognitive load, Cognitive Load (intrinsic / extraneous), extraneous cognitive load, AI brain fry, Precision Proactivity, task switching (LLM behavior)

Tensions

Is the value question genuinely settled by evidence, or does uncertainty and sampling bias still dominate?
from: What did the participants of the Thoughtworks 'Future of Software Engineering' Europe retreat (mid-2026) conclude about how agentic AI is reshaping software engineering?
  • Notes from FOSE Europe (Giles Edwards-Alexander): The evidence is in; the value is clearly here and here to stay.
  • Finding Comfort in the Uncertainty (Annie Vella): Nobody has it figured out; there is more uncertainty than certainty, and the shared set of questions is the real outcome.
  • What Survived Contact (Sam Ruby): 'Rigor migrates' is about as settled as this young field gets, but the room is a non-random, incumbent-tilted sample, and the doubt is currently better evidenced than the hope.
Assessment: The single sharpest divergence, and it sits directly under the headline whether-to-how convergence: they narrate the same shift but disagree on what it establishes. Edwards-Alexander and Herlein read the room's confidence as evidence; Vella and Ruby read the same room as a self-selected in-group. Ruby has the better of it methodologically — he is the only one to interrogate the sample and to notice the evidence is asymmetric (upside anecdotal, downside studied), which is uncomfortable precisely because the cohort's confidence is built on the weaker side.
If code is no longer the bottleneck, what is the new binding constraint?
from: What did the participants of the Thoughtworks 'Future of Software Engineering' Europe retreat (mid-2026) conclude about how agentic AI is reshaping software engineering?
  • Finding Comfort in the Uncertainty (Annie Vella): Decision capacity and downstream absorption — 'humanity is not ready for this much software'; decision fatigue is becoming the real bottleneck.
  • Notes from FOSE Europe (Giles Edwards-Alexander): Learning is the constraint now, not engineering delivery.
  • We've Stopped Arguing About Whether AI Changes Software (Greg Herlein): Knowledge — the tacit, unwritten 'why' behind past decisions — is the scarce resource, not code.
Assessment: They agree code/delivery has ceased to be the constraint but name three different successors, with different investment implications — expand decision-making capacity (Vella), optimise for learning (Edwards-Alexander), or capture tacit knowledge (Herlein). These are complementary in principle but rivalrous for attention and budget; the disagreement matters because each implies a different 'first thing to fix.'
Does AI adoption improve output and developer experience together, or do they decouple — with more output bought at the cost of the developers?
from: What is the future of software development in the agentic/AI age?
  • Jimmy Nilsson's Post — reflections after the Thoughtworks 'Future of Software Development' retreat (Jimmy Nilsson): Harmony: adoption is energizing and the human element flourishes ('Extreme Humanism'); reasons for optimism.
  • Why did Martin Fowler invite 60 engineers to a ski resort? (Ivett Ördög): Liberation: adoption frees engineers from CRUD toil to focus on hard problems — 'exciting times.'
  • The Future Of Software Engineering (Thoughtworks): Decoupling: organizations can gain output while developers report lower satisfaction — productivity and experience are coming apart.
  • Finding Comfort in the Uncertainty (Annie Vella): Hidden cost: the engineers going hardest on AI are being exhausted by it, and the warning signs are easy to miss.
  • Fragments: February 13 (Martin Fowler (Feb 13)): Intensification risk: teams stay the same size and 'do much more,' but doing much more is exactly what drives burnout.
Assessment: This is the specific, evidence-bearing form of the optimism/pessimism split, and it cuts directly against any simple reading of 'DevEx = AgentEx' as good news. The harmony camp (Nilsson, Ördög) speaks from felt experience and in-group mood; the decoupling camp (Thoughtworks, Vella) and Fowler's own cited HBR study bring the harder data, and it shows output and wellbeing pulling apart. Notably Fowler holds both 'same-size teams do much more' and the burnout finding without resolving them — the single clearest internal contradiction in the corpus, and a sign the harmony framing is doing more reassuring than the evidence supports.
If code was never the bottleneck, what is the binding constraint now?
from: What is the future of software development in the agentic/AI age?
  • Finding Comfort in the Uncertainty (Annie Vella): Human decision capacity and downstream absorption — 'decision fatigue is becoming the real bottleneck.'
  • Notes from FOSE Europe (Giles Edwards-Alexander): Learning — 'learning is the constraint now, not engineering delivery.'
  • We've Stopped Arguing About Whether AI Changes Software (Greg Herlein): Knowledge — the tacit, unwritten 'why' is the scarce resource agents starve for.
Assessment: A productive three-way split downstream of the shared 'code was never the bottleneck' finding. They are not incompatible so much as differently located — decision capacity (Vella) is organisational, learning (Edwards-Alexander) is about the team's rate of understanding the problem, and knowledge (Herlein) is about capturing rationale. But they imply different investments: expand downstream absorption, restructure around learning, or build knowledge-capture pipelines. The corpus has not reconciled which is the true binding constraint, and each author generalizes from a different vantage (a retreat-floor convergence, an org-design frame, a knowledge-engineering frame).

But CLT has a 'desirable difficulty' tradition (intentional friction supporting learning), and serendipitous unsolicited surfacing — exploration that helps users find what they wouldn't have queried — is a real category. The paper conflates 'precision' with 'good' without engaging the exploratory case.
    Precision Proactivity: Measuring Cognitive Load in Real-World AI-Assisted Work — read next: van Merriënboer & Sweller 2005 on germane load (cited ref. 125) — the paper's exclusion of germane load from its operationalization is itself a theoretical commitment worth examining.
  • construct validity vs alternative load measuresThe paper relies entirely on transcript-derived computational proxies. NASA-TLX, dual-task secondary performance, physiological measures (fNIRS, pupillometry) are listed as future-work triangulation. The 3× EL:ICL ratio is a strong claim resting on whether the two composites have comparable measurement reliability.
    Precision Proactivity: Measuring Cognitive Load in Real-World AI-Assisted Work — read next: Babaei et al. 2025 on NASA-TLX limitations (cited ref. 54); Hirshfield et al. 2024 on fNIRS for cognitive load in multiple resources (cited ref. 69).
  • cost / latency / model-economics blind spotSubtask focus stacks, explicit scope elicitation, and navigable architectures all imply additional compute, latency, and interaction friction. The paper does not engage with vendor cost models, latency-vs-quality tradeoffs, or the operational realities of deploying precision-proactive systems.
    Precision Proactivity: Measuring Cognitive Load in Real-World AI-Assisted Work — read next: Xiao & Yang 2025 'Streaming, fast and slow: cognitive load-aware streaming for efficient LLM serving' (cited ref. 154) — the only operational-economics paper cited.
  • attention infrastructure absent (hidden-layer thread)The paper treats cognitive load as intra-dialogue. It does not engage with the broader attention environment users bring to the work (notifications, multitasking, fragmented focus — Bailey, Stolen Focus, Hyperfocus). Extraneous load in the conversation arrives on top of ambient extraneous load in the environment, and the paper's instruments cannot see the latter.
    Precision Proactivity: Measuring Cognitive Load in Real-World AI-Assisted Work — read next: Mark, Gudith & Klocke 2008 'cost of interrupted work' (cited ref. 36) is used only for intra-dialogue framing — Chris Bailey's Hyperfocus, Stolen Focus, and the broader attention-infrastructure literature would be the right complement.
  • skill acquisition under sustained AI use (Beane signature)The paper raises in §7.4.1 the long-term schema-acquisition concern: 'if the self-sustaining load patterns we document prevent novice users from constructing coherent task schemas during AI-assisted work, the long-term consequence may extend beyond diminished output quality to impaired skill development.' Proposes longitudinal study as future work but does not test. Given Beane is co-author and The Skill Code's central concern is preserving conditions for skill acquisition, this is the most productive incomplete line in the paper. The one-shot 10-hour design is structurally unable to address it.
    Precision Proactivity: Measuring Cognitive Load in Real-World AI-Assisted Work — read next: Matthew Beane, The Skill Code; van Merriënboer & Sweller 2005 on cognitive load and complex learning (cited ref. 125).
  • governance / policy framing latentMishkin's policy lens does not surface — proactivity is treated as a design problem, not a policy or organizational-adoption problem. The 'precision proactivity' implications could be read as soft regulatory guidance for high-stakes professional contexts but the paper does not develop this. The latent policy angle (should regulators require scope-elicitation in high-stakes professional AI?) is not posed.
    Precision Proactivity: Measuring Cognitive Load in Real-World AI-Assisted Work — read next: Appel et al. 2026 Anthropic Economic Index report (cited ref. 52) for the policy framing Mishkin and co-authors have produced elsewhere.
  • RLHF interpretation as mechanism vs analogyThe Discussion proposes that prompt→response spillover reflects RLHF optimizing for relevance and helpfulness. This is plausible but mechanistic only by analogy — the paper does not actually instrument the model or correlate with known RLHF training details. Newer post-training paradigms (Constitutional AI, RLAIF, weighted preference learning, reasoning models) may produce different mirroring behaviors.
    Precision Proactivity: Measuring Cognitive Load in Real-World AI-Assisted Work — read next: Examine whether reasoning-trained models (o-series, Claude 3.7+ extended thinking, GPT-5 reasoning modes) exhibit different prompt-mirroring spillover under the same measurement framework. Stiennon et al. 2020 and Ouyang et al. 2022 (refs. 110-111) are the cited RLHF foundations.
  • extension vs relabel of classical CLTThe paper claims to extend CLT by reframing extraneous load as dynamic and path-dependent. But classical CLT has dynamic formulations (effective intrinsic load varies with expertise via schema automation — Kalyuga et al. 2003 expertise reversal). The contribution is sharper if read as: importing CLT's static-design-feature framing into the dynamic conversational setting required computational instruments classical CLT didn't have, which then revealed the static framing was insufficient. Whether this is 'extension' or 'translation' is not foregrounded.
    Precision Proactivity: Measuring Cognitive Load in Real-World AI-Assisted Work — read next: Sweller 1994 (ref. 6); Kalyuga et al. 2003 expertise reversal (ref. 124); van Merriënboer & Sweller 2005 (ref. 125).
  • harness-engineering syntopical bridgeThe paper does not engage with the agent-harness literature (compaction, sub-agents, context anxiety) but its within-speaker persistence finding is precisely the empirical grounding that literature needs. Each compaction event in a harness can be read as an attempt to break the self-sustaining load trajectory documented here.
    Precision Proactivity: Measuring Cognitive Load in Real-World AI-Assisted Work — read next: Anthropic harness paper on long-running application development; Turing Post 5 patterns for long-running agents; Cloudflare compaction-loss anecdotes.
  • evidence gapThe f(K,P,T) formula and the 20% fog-out rate are presented as facts but without citation. The empathetic mirroring / mirror neuron hypothesis is self-labeled 'lunatic fringe' but treated as explanatory. Winston's Star is derived from a single self-referential case study.
    How to Speak — read next: Look into research on cognitive load theory (Sweller), dual-coding theory (Paivio), and the replication status of mirror neuron claims for more rigorous grounding.
