Architecture Decision Records

Lightweight written records of architectural decisions and their rationale — cheap at write time, invaluable to both future humans and cold-starting agents.

Also known as: ADR, ADRs, ADR (Architecture Decision Record), Architecture Decision Records (ADR), decision log / lightweight ADR, decision log

Documents (3)

  • The Intent Debt Addy Osmani (addyosmani.com) · 2026-06-06 source 🖥️
    Intent debt — the absence of externalized rationale, goals and constraints in artifacts an agent can actually read — is the one of Storey's three debts (technical, cognitive, intent) that AI agents structurally cannot pay down, because intent is the one input that must originate with a human; and because agents start every session cold with no tacit memory, every gap in the externalized why is now paid every session by every agent, making the previously-affordable habit of carrying intent in your head the single most expensive workflow choice in agent-assisted development.
  • 13) Tobias K. Torrissen om kodekvalitet, konferanser og AI Truls Jørgensen og Audun Fauchald Strand — Heis Podcast · 2026-03-16 source 🖥️
    In the age of LLM-generated code, the durable value of an engineering leader is not in writing code but in cultivating the engineering practices, explicit design discipline, and craft community that determine whether a long-lived system can be understood, maintained, and adapted — and code quality, understood as 'obvious over clever,' matters more in this era, not less.
  • 5) Vidar Moe: Det eneste Vidar vil er å ha det lett Truls Jørgensen og Audun Fauchald Strand — Heis Podcast · 2025-11-17 source 🖥️
    Vidar Moe's core engineering-leadership philosophy is 'gjøre det lett og rett' (make it easy and right) — a Toyota-Way-inflected craft and leadership stance in which you lead an organization most powerfully not by exhortation but by patiently building the tooling, frameworks, fagtid, hiring filters, fixed-term leadership rotation, and shared decision arenas that make the right thing the path of least resistance; the episode's ironic title ('the only thing Vidar wants is to have it easy') inverts this — Moe wants the work to be easy precisely because he is willing to do the very hard, decade-long, mostly invisible work of removing friction from doing the right thing, and his current concern with AI agents is the natural next chapter of the same project.
