Documents (8)

  • Is AI Doom Going Out of Style? AI Daily Brief · 2026-05-05 source 🖥️
    The AI job-doom narrative is losing its grip because intellectual pushback (Jevons' Paradox reaching mainstream audiences via Klein/Imas) and market validation (agentic revenue explosion, rising software job postings) are converging simultaneously — though the authors acknowledge the shift rests on thin evidence and public sentiment remains deeply negative.
  • The growing AI backlash Gary Marcus · 2026-05-04 source 🖥️
    Generative AI has been a net negative for society outside a few domains like coding, the backlash was predictable and is now accelerating toward political salience, and the industry bears primary responsibility for privatizing gains while externalizing harms.
  • Could generative AI turn out to be the tech industry's Vietnam? And could public backlash lead AI to a better path? Gary Marcus from Marcus on AI · 2026-05 source 🖥️
    A growing public backlash against generative AI — analogous to the anti-Vietnam movement — is poised to force a political repricing of America's multitrillion-dollar AI bet and can, if organized, redirect policy toward FDA-style pre-deployment regulation.
  • Why Enterprise AI Has a Leadership Problem AI Daily Brief · 2026-04-10 source 🖥️
    Enterprise AI adoption is surging in investment and tooling but failing organizationally because leaders are spending almost exclusively on infrastructure and models while neglecting the human, cultural, and structural changes required for adoption — making AI success a leadership problem, not a technology problem.
  • Kunstig intelligens gir neppe en ny, industriell revolusjon Tobias Lunde · 2026-04-10 source 🖥️
    History shows that AI is unlikely to produce an economic revolution comparable to the Industrial Revolution, because AI does not remove fundamental physical constraints on production the way steam and electricity did, because technology diffusion takes decades, and because broad value creation depends on institutional and infrastructural preconditions that are not yet in place.
  • Starving Genies Kent Beck · 2026-04-03 source 🖥️
    The simultaneous usage throttling by AI model providers is best understood not as an engineering constraint but as a narrative constraint: all three face the same investor-story shelf life, forcing synchronized demand-side rationing while the race to bend the supply curve up determines who wins the next wave.
  • AI's biggest critic has lost the plot Kelsey Piper · 2026-04-01 source 🖥️
    Ed Zitron's AI-bubble thesis has deteriorated from a defensible economic argument in 2024 into unfalsifiable fraud accusations in 2026, because he refused to update his conclusion when costs fell and adoption surged, and the public discourse desperately needs better-calibrated skepticism in its place.
  • Agents Over Bubbles Ben Thompson (Stratechery) · 2026-03 source 🖥️
    The emergence of AI agents — which integrate models with software harnesses and multiply the output of a small number of agentic humans — means the massive AI infrastructure investment is justified demand rather than speculative bubble, and positions integrated model-plus-harness companies (Anthropic, OpenAI) as the durable winners in the value chain.
