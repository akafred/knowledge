Open gaps (31)

  • incompleteThe underdetermination argument is applied to AI training without engaging with how neural networks actually generalize. In practice, inductive biases, architecture, and training dynamics constrain generalization far more than the pure logical argument suggests. The mathematical fact that infinitely many functions fit finite data does not mean a particular learning algorithm is equally likely to learn any of them.
    AI Alignment Is Impossible — read next: Literature on neural network generalization, the lottery ticket hypothesis, and why deep learning generalizes despite overparameterization (e.g., Zhang et al. 2017, Arora et al. 2019). Also mechanistic interpretability research that aims to verify what models have learned.
  • incompleteThe is-ought argument assumes AI cannot develop functional analogs to emotion or sympathy. It treats the Humean framework as requiring literal biological evolution, ignoring the possibility that sufficiently complex information processing systems might develop emergent motivational states or that sympathy could be architecturally encoded.
    AI Alignment Is Impossible — read next: Affective computing, computational models of empathy, functionalist philosophy of mind (e.g., can functional states play the role of emotions?). Also work on reward modeling as a form of value encoding.
  • incompleteThe article presents alignment as a binary — either it works perfectly or it doesn't work at all. It does not engage with the possibility of 'good enough' alignment for practically safe AI, risk mitigation through layered defenses, or alignment as an ongoing process rather than a one-time achievement.
    AI Alignment Is Impossible — read next: AI governance literature, defense-in-depth approaches to AI safety, Christiano's work on iterated amplification, and Anthropic's work on constitutional AI as pragmatic (not theoretically perfect) alignment.
  • uninformedThe article does not engage with any current alignment research beyond a vague reference to reward/punishment training.
