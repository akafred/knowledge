Coaching Styles (Situational)

Matching a manager's coaching approach — directive, autonomy-granting, question-led, or collaborative — to an employee's experience level and the task's urgency/complexity.

Also known as: instructive coaching, hands-off coaching, asking/listening coaching, collaborative coaching, situational leadership, directive vs supportive coaching

Documents (1)

  • Our Favorite Management Tips on Giving Feedback HBR Editors · 2026-05-21 Reader 🖥️
    Feedback works when it separates the person from the behavior and is delivered inside a relationship the recipient already trusts: define what feedback must never do (attack character), structure it around a specific behavior plus its impact plus a next step, invite it proactively rather than only issuing it top-down, calibrate its intensity and timing to the relationship's maturity and the issue's severity, and treat it as a two-way, purpose-conferring conversation rather than a one-way verdict. The eight tips are different tactical answers to one underlying claim: feedback's effectiveness is gated by psychological safety and behavioral specificity, not by the giver's honesty or courage alone.
